<p><a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/aec/jaime-rosales.html">By Jaime Rosales</a> (@<a href="https://twitter.com/AfroJme">afrojme</a>)</p>  <p>TT Core Studio hosted their annual AEC Hackathon, the last weekend of September, and I was invited to join them as one of the Hackathon Leaders to help the participants with their new AEC app creations. Two days of hacking, pizza and red bull happened, delivering successful creation from the participants. I had a chance to show the LMV technology that Autodesk is promoting to make your web site 3D enabled, which also allows you to extract the valuable meta-data from your 3D models, and also, to partially join a team, while I helped others.</p>  <p><a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/.a/6a0167607c2431970b01b7c7dadb80970b-pi"><font size="3"><img title="Screen Shot 2015-10-08 at 4.23.05 PM" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="Screen Shot 2015-10-08 at 4.23.05 PM" src="/assets/image_762614.jpg" width="463" height="183" /></font></a></p>  <p><font size="3"></font></p>  <p>I will share with you a small summary of three apps that caught my eye and since I had a chance to help them or just heard their plan, I felt more identified with.    <br />The first one will be the one I got to spend more time with, Augmented Reality BIM, the idea came out from the IKEA IPad app to place your new furniture with AR in your house, so together with a group of TT Core well known developers the hacking started. We even got that excited that we decided to trademark the idea and get the domain name for our project, check it out <a href="http://www.augmentedbim.com">AugmentedBIM.com</a>. It was definitely a fun experience to code along new awesome friends</p>  <p><a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/.a/6a0167607c2431970b01b7c7dadb88970b-pi"><font size="3"><img title="Screen Shot 2015-10-08 at 4.17.54 PM" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="Screen Shot 2015-10-08 at 4.17.54 PM" src="/assets/image_919559.jpg" width="454" height="237" /></font></a></p>  <p><font size="3">The second one, who also won the Hackathon was the app called DOCQR using the Open Source Spectacles viewer and Revit API. The idea is to generate an open source QR code generator, that takes care of creating the URL to link to in order to view the model, and display it with Spectacles. Curious about it? you can read more about it </font><a href="http://devpost.com/software/docqr"><font size="3">here</font></a><font size="3">. </font></p>  <p><a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/.a/6a0167607c2431970b01b8d164d0fc970c-pi" target="_blank"><font size="3"><a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/.a/6a0167607c2431970b01b7c7dadb98970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Docqr" style="border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px" border="0" alt="Docqr" src="/assets/image_620965.jpg" width="447" height="336" /></a></font></a></p>  <p><font size="3">And the third one (secretly my favorite) was a set of plugins developed for Revit and Rhino that enable them to communicate with team members via Slack. This allows the model to alert users if something goes awry and giving a programmable interface for model management and maintenance. It is already available for you to play with and </font><a href="http://devpost.com/software/slackit"><font size="3">here</font></a><font size="3"> is also more description about it and the </font><a href="https://github.com/pix3lot/Slackit"><font size="3">link</font></a><font size="3"> to their Github Repo. </font></p>  <p><font size="3"></font></p>  <p><font size="3"></font></p>  <p><a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/.a/6a0167607c2431970b01b7c7dadb9a970b-pi" target="_blank"><font size="3"><a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/.a/6a0167607c2431970b01b7c7dadb9e970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="giphy worksharing Revit" style="border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px" border="0" alt="giphy worksharing Revit" src="/assets/image_403749.jpg" width="364" height="259" /></a></font></a><a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/.a/6a0167607c2431970b01bb087ee0e2970d-pi" target="_blank"><font size="3"><a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/.a/6a0167607c2431970b01b8d164d104970c-pi" target="_blank"><img title="synching slack" style="border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px" border="0" alt="synching slack" src="/assets/image_775244.jpg" width="368" height="262" /></a></font></a></p>  <p><font size="3">Definitely it was a great weekend, You can check out the rest of the project’s </font><a href="http://aec-technology-hackathon-2015.devpost.com/submissions"><font size="3">here</font></a><font size="3">.</font></p>  <p><font size="3"></font></p>  <p><font size="3">Back to Revit API, this week we hosted Revit Answer Days, which was a total success with over 900’ish posts, the only downside for us ADN is that we only had around 10 API questions, but I guess it was ok for being the first one.
