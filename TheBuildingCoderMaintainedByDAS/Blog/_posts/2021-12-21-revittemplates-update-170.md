<p>Roman <a href="https://github.com/Nice3point">Nice3point</a> made
huge contributions to RevitLookup in the past few months.</p>

<p>Today, on this shortest day of the year (up here in the northern hemisphere), he would appreciate your feedback
on <a href="https://github.com/Nice3point/RevitTemplates/discussions/19">update 1.7.0</a> of another of his projects,
<a href="https://github.com/Nice3point/RevitTemplates">RevitTemplates</a>:</p>

<p>Last big update in the outgoing year.</p>

<ul>
<li>.Net SDK 6 support</li>
<li>Nuke 6 support</li>
<li>C# 10 support</li>
<li>Implicit usings</li>
<li>File-scoped namespaces</li>
<li>The revit-sln template is now displayed in the IDE</li>
<li>Added group Solution Items with frequently used non-project files</li>
<li>Added SubTransaction</li>
<li>Added extensions for adding images to a ribbon</li>
<li>Added copying .pdb files to Revit folder for non-Rider debugger</li>
<li>Optimized .csproj file for revit-addin template. Added generate Assembly Information</li>
<li>Replaced Nuget packages with floating versions</li>
<li>YML files switched to windows-2022 image</li>
<li>Bin folder is no longer displayed in explorer</li>
<li>The installer is now an optional parameter.</li>
<li>Fixed changelog parser</li>
<li>Other minor changes and improvements</li>
</ul>

<p>Visual Studio 2022 supports custom parameters, create projects without using a terminal.
Just remember when creating a solution to check "Put solution and project in same directory".
VS can also change the preconfigured solution configurations, be careful and read WIKI.</p>
