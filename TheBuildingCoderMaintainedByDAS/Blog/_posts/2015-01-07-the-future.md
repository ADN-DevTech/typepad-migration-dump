<p>In these troubled times, I was struck by this WWI poem sent out for the New Year by my bookshop and literature friend <a href="http://buch-metzler.de">Franz Metzler</a>:</p>

<a name="2"></a>

<h4>L’Avenir</h4>

<blockquote>
<p>Soulevons la paille
<br/>regardons la neige
<br/>écrivons des lettres
<br/>attendons des ordres</p>

<p>Fumons la pipe
<br/>en songeant à l‘amour
<br/>les gabions sont là
<br/>regardons la rose</p>

<p>La fontaine n’a pas tari
<br/>pas plus que l’or de la paille ne s’est terni
<br/>regardons l’abeille
<br/>et ne songeons pas à l’avenir</p>

<p>Regardons nos mains
<br/>qui sont la neige
<br/>la rose et l’abeille
<br/>ainsi que l’avenir</p>
</blockquote>

<p style="text-align: right"><i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guillaume_Apollinaire">Guillaume Apollinaire</a> (1880 – 1918)</i></p>


<a name="3"></a>

<h4>The Future</h4>

<blockquote>
<p>We lift the straw
<br/>look at the snow
<br/>write letters
<br/>await orders</p>

<p>We smoke our pipes
<br/>dreaming of love
<br/>gabions are up
<br/>admiring the roses</p>

<p>The fountain has not run dry
<br/>nor waned the gold of the straw
<br/>We look at the bee
<br/>don't think about future</p>

<p>We look at our hands
<br/>that are the snow
<br/>the rose and the bee
<br/>as well as the future</p>
</blockquote>

<p style="text-align: right"><i>Translation by Jeremy Tammik</i></p>
