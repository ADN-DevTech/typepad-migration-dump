<h4>My New Near Field Communication Business Card</h4>

<p>We have been handing out Google cardboard boxes at our meetup events.</p>

<p>Each box includes an

<a href="http://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Near_field_communication">
NFC</a> card.

Simply hold it against the back of your NFC-enabled phone and it will take you straight to the <a href="http://www.dodocase.com">Dodocase</a> web site.</p>

<center>

<a class="asset-img-link"  style="display: inline;" href="http://thebuildingcoder.typepad.com/.a/6a00e553e16897883301bb07c5d421970d-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, '_blank', 'width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0' ); return false"><img class="asset  asset-image at-xid-6a00e553e16897883301bb07c5d421970d img-responsive" alt="Dodocase NFC card" title="Dodocase NFC card" src="/assets/image_00cfd6.jpg" /></a><br />

</center>

<p>You can easily reprogram it to take you to any other URL you like, in just 60 seconds, e.g. using the free

<a href="http://www.tagstand.com">Tagstand NFC Writer</a>.

There are lots of other free NFC write apps in the mobile stores as well.</p>

<p>I grabbed a card and set it up to link to

<a href="http://thebuildingcoder.typepad.com/blog/about-the-author.html">
About the Author</a> on

The Building Coder.</p>

<p>No more paper business cards for me, just a few electrons.</p>

<p>Sustainable.</p>

<p>And cool &nbsp; :-)</p>
