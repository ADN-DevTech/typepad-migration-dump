<p>By&#0160;<a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/manufacturing/adam-nagy.html" target="_self">Adam Nagy</a></p>
<p><strong>Inventor 2016</strong> introduced a new security measure, which makes the <strong>Add-In Manager</strong> pop up a dialog when it encounters an <strong>Add-In</strong> for the <strong>first time</strong>:</p>
<p><a class="asset-img-link" href="http://a4.typepad.com/6a0112791b8fe628a401bb0822f8ac970d-pi" style="display: inline;"><img alt="AddInMessage" class="asset  asset-image at-xid-6a0112791b8fe628a401bb0822f8ac970d img-responsive" src="/assets/image_3fb491.jpg" title="AddInMessage" /></a>&#0160;</p>
<p>Here if you click <strong>Allow</strong> then from then onwards the <strong>Add-In</strong> will automatically be loaded without the message box appearing again. If you click <strong>Block</strong> then the <strong>Add-In</strong> will never be loaded until you un-tick the <strong>Block</strong> option for it in the <strong>Add-In Manager&#0160;</strong>dialog:</p>
<p><a class="asset-img-link" href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/.a/6a0167607c2431970b01b7c77ef6a8970b-popup" onclick="window.open( this.href, &#39;_blank&#39;, &#39;width=640,height=480,scrollbars=no,resizable=no,toolbar=no,directories=no,location=no,menubar=no,status=no,left=0,top=0&#39; ); return false" style="display: inline;"><img alt="AddInManager" class="asset  asset-image at-xid-6a0167607c2431970b01b7c77ef6a8970b img-responsive" src="/assets/image_e6d254.jpg" title="AddInManager" /></a></p>
<p>The <strong>Add-In</strong> security message box also contains <strong>Signature</strong>, <strong>Publisher</strong> and <strong>Detail</strong> sections that are populated from the <strong>digital signature</strong> of the <strong>Add-In</strong>. Unlike in case of <strong>AutoCAD</strong>, inside <strong>Inventor</strong> the warning dialog will pop up even if the <strong>Add-In</strong> is signed, but at least the <strong>digital signature</strong> information will be there, which makes the <strong>Add-In</strong> look more professional.</p>
<p><strong>Autodesk</strong> does not provide its own digital signature/certificate for 3rd party developers, because that would make the <strong>Add-Ins</strong> look as if they were developed by <strong>Autodesk</strong>, which is not correct. Developers who wish to sign their <strong>Add-Ins</strong> need to purchase their own signature/certificate. They can get a single one and used it for all their products.</p>
