<p>By <a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/manufacturing/vladimir-ananyev.html" target="_self">Vladimir Ananyev</a></p>  <p><b></b></p>  <p><b>Issue</b></p>  <p>How to set the color of a part in conjunction with a desired material?</p>  <p><a name="section2"></a><b>Solution</b></p>  <p>You can specify both the color (using the RenderStyle object) and material (using the Material object) of a part using the Inventor API. A newly created part is created with the default color and material both of which can be overridden. Assigning a color to the part would mean that it retains its default material but, a new color is assigned. This would be equivalent to keeping the default material of the part but, coloring the outside of the part. </p>  <p>Next, if you were to assign a material to the part, since the material has its own default color, the previously assigned color will now be overridden. But, the Inventor API also allows you to override the default color of a material. Hence, before assigning a material to a part, you can reset the color of the material, and then assign the material to the part. Another method would be, to assign the desired material to a part (without customizing its color) and then assign the desired color to the part itself. <b>But</b> the Material (also like other styles: RenderStyle, Drawing style etc.) can be edited when it is a local one(or both in library and local document).</p>  <p>&#160;</p>  <h1 style="line-height: 16pt;
