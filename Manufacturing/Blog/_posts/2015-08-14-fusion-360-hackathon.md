<p>By&#0160;<a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/manufacturing/adam-nagy.html" target="_self">Adam Nagy</a></p>
<p>Just mentioned it a few weeks ago that the online&#0160;<a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/manufacturing/2015/07/fusion-c-api-and-fusion-hackathon.html" target="_self">Fusion&#0160;360&#0160;Hackathon</a> is coming in <strong>September</strong>. This is just a reminder :)</p>
<p><a href="http://www.autodesk.co.uk/products/fusion-360/overview" target="_self">Fusion 360</a> has now 3 programming languages you can choose from: <strong>JavaScript</strong>, <strong>Python</strong> and <strong>C++<br /></strong>You can get started with the <strong>API</strong>&#0160;<a href="http://autodeskfusion360.github.io" target="_self">here</a>.</p>
<p>If you are looking for ideas concerning what to program then it&#39;s worth having a look at the <a href="http://forums.autodesk.com/t5/ideas/v2/ideaexchangepage/blog-id/125" target="_self">Fusion&#0160;360&#0160;IdeaStation</a> requests - that&#39;s where we got our ideas from for the sample apps that we published on the <a href="https://apps.exchange.autodesk.com/FUSION/en/Home/Index" target="_self">Fusion&#0160;360&#0160;App&#0160;Store</a>. They all come with source code so you could also build on them.</p>
<p>Good luck! &#0160;</p>
