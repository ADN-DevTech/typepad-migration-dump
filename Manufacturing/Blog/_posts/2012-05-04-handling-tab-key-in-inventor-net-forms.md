<p>By <a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/manufacturing/philippe-leefsma.html" target="_self">Philippe Leefsma</a></p>  <p>When displaying a custom .Net form, typically during a command to gather inputs or allow the user to control your command’s behavior, you may have noticed than your form will not honor the tab indices you may have defined when designing the form under Visual Studio editor. Actually pressing the Tab key will have no effect.</p>  <p>The reason is that Inventor is setting some low level hook mechanism to intercept keypress events such as Tab or Enter, so you won’t be able to directly redirect those events to your controls unless you use some low level and probably complex workarounds.</p>  <p>The code proposed below illustrates a way to handle and respond to Tab keypress events with some custom logic.
