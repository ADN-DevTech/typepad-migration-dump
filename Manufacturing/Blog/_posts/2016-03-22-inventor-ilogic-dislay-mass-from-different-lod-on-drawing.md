<p>By <a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/manufacturing/xiaodong-liang.html" target="_self">Xiaodong Liang</a></p>  <p>This is from a <a href="http://forums.autodesk.com/t5/inventor-customization/mass-from-different-levels-of-detail/m-p/6217571#M62402">forum post</a> in which one customer wanted to get mass of each LOD and display them on the drawing of top assembly.&#160; </p>  <p>The advocate <a href="http://forums.autodesk.com/t5/user/viewprofilepage/user-id/1720471">Rossano Praderi</a> shared a solution which is nice to me. He provided the dataset only. I think it will be helpful for more customers with the workflow and codes out of the dataset. So I wrote this blog. Thanks <a href="http://forums.autodesk.com/t5/user/viewprofilepage/user-id/1720471">Rossano Praderi</a>!</p>  <p>1. In the attached dataset, there is a top assembly with several components. Three LOD (First, Second, Third) are created. </p>  <p>2. The corresponding drawing of the top assembly is also created.</p>  <p><a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/.a/6a0167607c2431970b01b8d1b25586970c-pi"><img style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="clip_image001" border="0" alt="clip_image001" src="/assets/image_90114b.jpg" width="244" height="126" /></a></p>  <p>3.
