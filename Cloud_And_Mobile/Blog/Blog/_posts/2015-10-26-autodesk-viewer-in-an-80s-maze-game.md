<p>By&#0160;<a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/cloud_and_mobile/adam-nagy.html" target="_self">Adam Nagy</a></p>
<p>I was at <a href="http://www.hackmanchester.com/" target="_self">HackManchester</a> last weekend with my colleagues from our Sheffield office where one of the teams created an 80&#39;s style game where you have to find your way out of a maze.</p>
<p>They generate a random maze and its 3D geometry and then transform it to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/STL_(file_format)" target="_self">STL</a> which can be uploaded to our <a href="https://developer.autodesk.com/api/view-and-data-api/" target="_self">View and Data</a> server. Then our viewer is used to show the maze and can be controlled by keyboard arrow keys or from an iPhone.</p>
<p><iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/w-a7FxbBmLU" width="560"></iframe></p>
<p>You can find the game <a href="http://auto-maize.herokuapp.com" target="_self">here</a>&#0160;(best on Chrome) and the iPhone controller <a href="http://auto-maize.herokuapp.com/controller.html" target="_self">here</a>&#0160;</p>
<p>We liked it so much that they got the Autodesk prize for it.</p>
<p>Congratulations <strong>Team PsyPeg</strong>, aka Pez and Ollie! :)</p>
