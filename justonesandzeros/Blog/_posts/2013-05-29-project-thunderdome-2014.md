<p><strong>Update</strong>: <a href="http://justonesandzeros.typepad.com/blog/2014/05/project-thunderdome-2015-now-with-data-standard.html" target="_self">Thunderdome 2015</a> has been released.</p>
<p>Wow.&#0160; I can’t believe I’m on the 4th release of Thunderdome.&#0160; They grow up so fast.&#0160; If you are not familiar with the app, Project Thunderdome lets you deploy various Vault settings to all your Vault users.&#0160; Settings include, shortcuts, saved searches, grid settings, <a href="http://justonesandzeros.typepad.com/blog/2013/04/deco-2014.html">DECO</a> UI, and even Vault plug-ins.</p>
<p><img alt="" src="/assets/deployment.png" /></p>
<p>This time around I just ported the app to 2014 and didn’t add any new features.&#0160; So have a look at the <a href="http://justonesandzeros.typepad.com/blog/2012/08/project-thunderdome-2013.html">Thunderdome 2013</a> to get a feel of the full feature set.&#0160; Looking over the 2013 comments, some people mentioned that the Grid State only applied to the main grid.&#0160; I tested things out in 2014 and Grid State seems to also apply to the default tabs, such as History.&#0160;</p>
