<p>Not all blog posts are equal.&nbsp; Some posts contain critical information that every Vault developer should know, while other posts are only relevant to specialized areas of Vault development.&nbsp; This page is here to highlight the posts with important information that everyone should be aware of.</p>
<p><a href="http://justonesandzeros.typepad.com/blog/2012/09/impersonation-is-deprecated.html">Impersonation is Deprecated</a><br />The impersonation feature in the Vault 2013 API will not be supported in the next release.</p>
<p><a href="http://justonesandzeros.typepad.com/blog/2011/06/2-api-related-hotfixes.html" target="_self">2 API-Related Hotfixes</a><br /><a href="http://justonesandzeros.typepad.com/blog/2011/04/issues-with-the-vault-2012-api.html" target="_self">Issues with the Vault 2012 API</a><br />Error and hotfix information for the Vault 2012 API.</p>
<p><a href="http://justonesandzeros.typepad.com/blog/2011/02/net-40.html" target="_self">.NET 4.0</a><br />If you are building an extension for Vault 2011 or 2012, you need to make sure you build with .NET 3.5.</p>
<p><a href="http://justonesandzeros.typepad.com/blog/2010/03/file-transfer-as-binary-data.html" target="_self">File Transfer as Binary Data</a><br />By default, files are converted to text during upload and download.&nbsp; This article explains how to configure your application for binary transfer, which is much quicker.</p>
<p><a href="http://justonesandzeros.typepad.com/blog/2009/11/errors-and-restrictions.html" target="_self">Errors and Restrictions</a><br />An explanation on how the Vault server communicates problems back to the client.</p>
