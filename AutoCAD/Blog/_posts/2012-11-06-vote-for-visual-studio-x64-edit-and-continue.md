I’m sure this isn’t the first time this topic has been raised with Microsoft – but hopefully it will be the last <img style="border-bottom-style: none; border-right-style: none; border-top-style: none; border-left-style: none" class="wlEmoticon wlEmoticon-winkingsmile" alt="Winking smile" src="/assets/image_126844.jpg" />. I went ahead and added my votes. And what’s a blog for, if not to nudge your readers into helping each other out once in a while <img style="border-bottom-style: none; border-right-style: none; border-top-style: none; border-left-style: none" class="wlEmoticon wlEmoticon-smile" alt="Smile" src="/assets/image_883834.jpg" />.</p>  <p>I know of many programmers who use Windows 32-bit on their dev machines purely for this feature in Visual Studio. Make your vote count.</p>
