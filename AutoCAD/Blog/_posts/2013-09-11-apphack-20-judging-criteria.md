<p><strong>Who is the Jury?</strong></p>
<p>A panel of Autodesk judges from various disciplines.</p>
<p><strong>How will the teams moving to the second round and to the final round be selected?</strong></p>
<p>A panel of Autodesk judges will select (10) teams moving to the second round, (5) teams moving to the final round and the winners of the contest at the closing event on December 2nd.</p>
<p><strong>Round One&nbsp; </strong></p>
<p><strong>Judging Criteria (Maximum of 15 points):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Ability to be present in Las Vegas to do a live demo of the app on December 2<sup>nd</sup>, 2013</li>
<li>Utility/Fun – whether a user will want to use the application again and
again:&nbsp; (Maximum: 5 points)</li>
<li>Coherence – whether the application process or workflow has a logical
set of steps to be executed and the application benefits to the end user are
clear.(Maximum: 5 points)</li>
<li>Adherence of the application to the theme,
Connecting Autodesk to the Cloud (Maximum: 5 points)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Round Two and Final Round Judging Criteria (Maximum&nbsp;of 40 points):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Utility/Fun – whether a user will want to use the application again and
again:&nbsp; (Maximum:&nbsp;10 points) </li>
<li>Design – The look, feel and usability of the application: (Maximum: 5 points)</li>
<li>Innovation – How original is the idea and execution: (Maximum:&nbsp;10 points)</li>
<li>Completeness – How complete is the Application? Are there bugs or dead
ends?:&nbsp; (Maximum: 5 points)</li>
<li>Adherence of the application to the theme,
Connecting Autodesk to the Cloud (Maximum:&nbsp;10 points)</li>
</ul>
