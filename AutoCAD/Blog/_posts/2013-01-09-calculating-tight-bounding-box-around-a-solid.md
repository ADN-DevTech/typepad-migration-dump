<p>By <a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/autocad/gopinath-taget.html">Gopinath Taget</a></p>  <p>Consider this: When you calculate the box of a solid, or any other AcDbEntity, this box is aligned with the x,y,z axes. This box might be much larger than the smallest box which could contain the solid. To take an extreme example, consider the cylinder with radius 1, height 100, aligned with the vector 1,1,1. The box returned by   <br />AutoCAD for this entity has the volume 2.092463E+005. The optimal box (not aligned with the x,y,z axes) has the volume 400. How can you obtain this box?</p>  <p>An exact answer for this problem is not simple, but a good approximation is possible. One can calculate the principal axes from the solid, and align the box with these axes. This gives the perfect answer in this case, and will generally produce a much better answer than doing nothing at all.
