<p>After taking <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2013/08/project-memento-now-available-on-autodesk-labs.html" target="_blank">a look at Project Memento</a>, last week, using it <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2013/08/using-project-memento-to-manipulate-mesh-output-from-recap-photo.html" target="_blank">to manipulate a mesh</a> I’d <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2013/06/comparing-123d-catch-with-recap-photo.html" target="_blank">previously created with ReCap Photo</a>, I decided to try out the latest update to the latter of these two tools.</p>  <p>The first thing to notice with this new, improved version of ReCap Photo is that it has <a href="http://recap.autodesk.com" target="_blank">its own portal showing your active projects</a>:<a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201901edf0c6d970b-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="The new ReCap Photo website" border="0" alt="The new ReCap Photo website" src="/assets/image_304757.jpg" width="450" height="425" /></a></p>  <p>The tool itself works in the same way: you simply select multiple photos in Autodesk 360 and select “Create 3D model” from the action menu. In fact, all I had to do to try this new version was to resubmit the photo scene via the .RCP file and wait for the results to come back:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20192ac9e5e1a970d-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Resubmitting the photo scene" border="0" alt="Resubmitting the photo scene" src="/assets/image_960807.jpg" width="450" height="390" /></a></p>  <p>And the results do seem better: the quality of the mesh I get back – such as the detail on the wheels and especially the car’s interior – is certainly much improved over the prior version.</p>  <p>I downloaded the hi-res .OBJ mesh, opened it in Project Memento and started fooling around with “detect issues”, using the automatic capabilities for fixing them.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201901edf0d14970b-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="A minor hole on the edge of the cropped mesh" border="0" alt="A minor hole on the edge of the cropped mesh" src="/assets/image_535735.jpg" width="464" height="253" /></a></p>        <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2019104d4fdf1970c-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="A larger hole in the footwell" border="0" alt="A larger hole in the footwell" src="/assets/image_148762.jpg" width="464" height="253" /></a></p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201901edf0e34970b-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="We don&#39;t want the wing mirrors removed, though" border="0" alt="We don&#39;t want the wing mirrors removed, though" src="/assets/image_147741.jpg" width="464" height="253" /></a></p>  <p>The detection tools seem fairly robust, although I admit to getting a bit confused when presented with defects that I’d already fixed when navigating through the list (I then managed to unfix a number of them by mistake). This is quite possibly down to user error, of course.</p>  <p>If you’re interested in the area of 3D capture, be sure to take the opportunity to try both <a href="http://recap.autodesk.com" target="_blank">ReCap Photo</a> and <a href="http://labs.autodesk.com/utilities/memento" target="_blank">Project Memento</a> while they’re still available for free (in exchange for providing us with greatly appreciated feedback :-).</p>
