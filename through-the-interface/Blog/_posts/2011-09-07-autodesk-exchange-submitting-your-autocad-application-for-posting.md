<p>In <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2011/09/autodesk-exchange-preparing-your-autocad-application-for-posting.html" target="_blank">the last post</a>, we saw the steps involved in preparing an AutoCAD application for posting to the Autodesk Exchange store. In this post, we’ll step through the submission process.</p>  <p>Let’s start with some basics: posting to Autodesk Exchange currently happens from within the AutoCAD product, which means you’ll need AutoCAD 2012 installed (and running) to submit your product.</p>  <h3>Step 1 – sign into (or up for and then into) Autodesk Exchange.</h3>  <p>Within AutoCAD 2012, sign in to Autodesk Exchange.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201543538e6af970c-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Sign in with your Autodesk ID" border="0" alt="Sign in with your Autodesk ID" src="/assets/image_968402.jpg" width="398" height="149" /></a></p>  <p>If you’re not already in possession of an Autodesk ID, click on the link to create one. If you have one, just use it to sign in and then skip ahead to Step 2.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2015391657da0970b-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Get an Autodesk ID" border="0" alt="Get an Autodesk ID" src="/assets/image_107124.jpg" width="169" height="163" /></a></p>  <p>You’ll need to fill out some information to get an Autodesk ID. I’ve gone through the process of creating a new one, for the purposes of this post (I wanted to show all the steps involved, rather than skipping ones I’d gone through in the past).</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2015391657dad970b-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Creating an Autodesk ID" border="0" alt="Creating an Autodesk ID" src="/assets/image_222981.jpg" width="301" height="353" /></a></p>  <p>Once your account is created, you’ll receive a notification in AutoCAD that you’ve been signed in automatically.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2014e8b592787970d-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Account created notification" border="0" alt="Account created notification" src="/assets/image_204171.jpg" width="322" height="126" /></a></p>  <h3>Step 2 – request your publisher status.</h3>  <p>Open Autodesk Exchange via the icon next to the notification, above. You’ll find the button to click on in order to start the process proper at the bottom-right of the Apps tab:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2014e8b5927bb970d-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Sign up button in Autodesk Exchange" border="0" alt="Sign up button in Autodesk Exchange" src="/assets/image_684127.jpg" width="447" height="319" /></a></p>  <p>You’ll be presented with an explanation of the concept behind Autodesk Exchange Apps and how to take advantage of it. Click on the “Publish Now” button at the bottom of the page, which will start the process of requesting the status of App Publisher.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201543538e73d970c-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Hit the publish your product button" border="0" alt="Hit the publish your product button" src="/assets/image_206203.jpg" width="425" height="319" /></a></p>  <p>Fill out the form with the pertinent details.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2014e8b592857970d-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Filling out the form" border="0" alt="Filling out the form" src="/assets/image_558132.jpg" width="398" height="322" /></a></p>  <p>There are several pages worth of agreement to read (and hopefully accept)…</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2015391657e29970b-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Accept the agreement" border="0" alt="Accept the agreement" src="/assets/image_539947.jpg" width="399" height="323" /></a></p>  <p>… via the radio-button at the bottom of the page.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201543538e7a2970c-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Having read every word" border="0" alt="Having read every word" src="/assets/image_848810.jpg" width="399" height="323" /></a></p>  <h3>Step 3 – fill out the product submission form.</h3>  <p>As you fill out this form – which includes quite a lot of information – I do recommend keeping a Notepad session going into which you copy &amp; paste the information you enter. This just makes it less likely that if a catastrophic error happens you’ll have to start from scratch.</p>  <p>Start by giving your application a name (which must be unique – you’ll be told if the name has already been taken when you try to preview the online help-file, later on), and then provide a short and long description of its functionality.</p>  <p>Use the “Choose File” button to select your ZIPped application bundle (as created in the previous post) and then “Upload” it.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2015391657e6e970b-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Start filling out the product information" border="0" alt="Start filling out the product information" src="/assets/image_653738.jpg" width="394" height="281" /></a></p>  <p>If you scroll down, you can specify the experience level required to use the application, the name and URL of your company as well as the email address to be used for support issues.</p>  <p>It’s here that you can specify the price of your application. If you select the “USD” option, you can enter the price you wish to charge. If you choose this option, you will need to provide details on your PayPal account (which you will need to set up separately – something beyond the scope of this particular post).</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2014e8b5928d6970d-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="More to fill out after uploading the package" border="0" alt="More to fill out after uploading the package" src="/assets/image_924958.jpg" width="394" height="281" /></a></p>  <p>Next you should select the type of product (the most likely choice is “App”, given the focus of this post) and its compatibility with AutoCAD-based products. Be sure that this compatibility reflects reality, otherwise you will certainly receive support issues related to incompatible products (as well as having disgruntled users), at some point in the future.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2014e8b5928f6970d-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="The pricing for the app as well as its compatibility" border="0" alt="The pricing for the app as well as its compatibility" src="/assets/image_466931.jpg" width="394" height="281" /></a></p>  <p>Next you should select the store categories. While this particular app is of general interest, I haven’t checked the various industries that might use it (all of them, I suspect). I selected “Productivity Tools”, and might have also selected “Translators”, but consider that as being more for model – rather than language – translation.</p>  <p>Lower down you can specify and upload a thumbnail image for your app and screenshots of it in action (images which need to be .jpg, .png of .gif – not .bmp).</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2015391657eb0970b-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Choose the industry, the type and upload images" border="0" alt="Choose the industry, the type and upload images" src="/assets/image_334506.jpg" width="394" height="281" /></a></p>  <p>Next you can add information for each command exposed by your application:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201543538e81c970c-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Add information about the commands" border="0" alt="Add information about the commands" src="/assets/image_628508.jpg" width="394" height="281" /></a></p>  <p>Followed by some general, descriptive text for the application itself:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201543538e867970c-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Some general information" border="0" alt="Some general information" src="/assets/image_493996.jpg" width="394" height="281" /></a></p>  <p>And then the final set of information around the author and the mechanism for support.</p>  <p>You can add information on the various versions, at this point, too, and then preview the HTML help that will get auto-generated using much of this content.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201543538e88d970c-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Preview the online help" border="0" alt="Preview the online help" src="/assets/image_15314.jpg" width="394" height="277" /></a></p>  <p>At this stage it’s important to check the preview of the online help for errors and correct them:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2014e8b592964970d-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Online help preview" border="0" alt="Online help preview" src="/assets/image_456329.jpg" width="394" height="277" /></a></p>  <p>And then hit submit, to send it across to us:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2014e8b59296e970d-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Hit submit when ready" border="0" alt="Hit submit when ready" src="/assets/image_584667.jpg" width="394" height="277" /></a></p>  <p>At which point you’ll receive a notification that the submission has occurred, all being well:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201543538e89e970c-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="We&#39;re done" border="0" alt="We&#39;re done" src="/assets/image_730234.jpg" width="394" height="281" /></a></p>  <p>I also received an email describing the successful submission:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2014e8b592986970d-pi" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Email confirmation of successful submission" border="0" alt="Email confirmation of successful submission" src="/assets/image_594781.jpg" width="422" height="272" /></a></p>  <p>In the next post, we’ll take a look at the process to follow should your application be rejected by the Autodesk Exchange Apps testing team (many applications end up needing an iteration or two of this process, for one reason or another).</p>
