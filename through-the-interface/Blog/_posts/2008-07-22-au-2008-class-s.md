<p>The class schedule for <a href="http://au.autodesk.com/event/">Autodesk University 2008</a> has been published on Joseph Wurcher's <a href="http://au.autodesk.com/blogs/view/2008_Session_Catalog/">BLAUG</a>. Here are this year's Developer Track sessions (although they may end up being categorized under &quot;Customization &amp; Programming&quot;, along with a host of other interesting classes from external presenters). You'll see a number of speakers listed, both from my team (DevTech) and our various Engineering teams (who I've highlighted in <em>italics</em> below).</p>

<p>I'm personally presenting three classes: two will be more-or-less the same as last year (here are the handouts for <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2007/10/au-handouts-the.html">There's More to DWG™ than AutoCAD®, Parts 1</a>, <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2007/10/au-handouts-t-1.html">2</a> and <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2007/10/au-handouts-t-2.html">3</a> and <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2007/10/au-handouts-enr.html">Enrich Your DWF™, Parts 1</a> and <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2007/10/au-handouts-e-1.html">2</a>), and the third is a new topic, focusing on using F# inside AutoCAD. I'll also be part of the panel for &quot;AutoCAD® APIs: Meet the Experts&quot;, which was (in my opinion) one of the best sessions at last year's event.</p>

<p>I'm looking forward to meeting Through-the-Interface readers at this year's event - do drop me a line if you're attending and would like to try and find some time to meet up.<span style="font-size: 0.6em;">&nbsp;</span></p>
