<p>Being a bit of a Star Wars fan, I was delighted to come across <a href="http://toddblatt.blogspot.com/2013/01/scout-trooper-blaster.html" target="_blank">this blog post</a> by former <a href="http://www.makerbot.com" target="_blank">MakerBot</a> employee Todd Blatt (now working at <a href="http://www.custom3dstuff.com" target="_blank">Custom 3D Stuff</a>), who modeled an Imperial Scout Trooper Blaster in AutoCAD for 3D printing.</p>  <p>You can download the STL output and the original DWG file from <a href="http://www.thingiverse.com/thing:42811" target="_blank">here</a>. Here’s a view on the finished gun with the “shades of gray” visual style:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2017d40a3286c970c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Imperial Scout Blaster inside AutoCAD - shades of gray" border="0" height="337" src="/assets/image_105133.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Imperial Scout Blaster inside AutoCAD - shades of gray" width="394" /></a></p>  <p>And here’s one that’s “realistic” with a nice metallic material applied to it:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2017c3674af59970b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Imperial Scout Blaster inside AutoCAD - realistic" border="0" height="269" src="/assets/image_452754.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="Imperial Scout Blaster inside AutoCAD - realistic" width="394" /></a></p>  <p>3D printing <em>functional</em>&#0160;<a href="http://www.extremetech.com/extreme/133514-the-worlds-first-3d-printed-gun" target="_blank">weapon</a> <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/andygreenberg/2013/01/14/gunsmiths-3d-print-high-capacity-ammo-clips-to-thwart-proposed-gun-laws/" target="_blank">parts</a> is clearly a contentious subject – which, according to some, may sadly lead to <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2013/01/18/like-it-or-not-i-think-3d-printing-is-about-to-get-legislated/" target="_blank">the industry being legislated</a> – but all this is just a bit of harmless fun by <a href="http://www.501st.com" target="_blank">enthusiasts who like to dress up</a>.
