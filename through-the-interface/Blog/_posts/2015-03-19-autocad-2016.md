<p>It’s that time again! Over the coming days you’re going to be hearing lots about the next release of AutoCAD, codenamed “Maestro”.<a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b8d0f009fb970c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="AutoCAD 2016 makes a splash" border="0" height="249" src="/assets/image_490155.jpg" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" title="AutoCAD 2016 makes a splash" width="394" /></a></p>  <p>Before taking a look at AutoCAD 2016 from a developer’s perspective – which we’ll do next time – in this post we’re going to take a quick look at its user features.</p>  <p>To kick things off here are <a href="http://help.autodesk.com/view/ACD/2016/ENU/?guid=GUID-B93A458E-1A7F-4090-A8CF-87A31C24E404" target="_blank">the new commands and system variables</a>, here are <a href="http://help.autodesk.com/view/ACD/2016/ENU/?guid=GUID-4FEBA606-95E0-4DC4-A116-257ED86DCD58" target="_blank">those that have changed</a> and here are <a href="http://help.autodesk.com/view/ACD/2016/ENU/?guid=GUID-5647ACE3-412E-48A6-8C1B-6B0EE09F5E3F" target="_blank">those that have been removed</a>. To cherry-pick from the top of the new commands list, CLOSEALLOTHER will close all open drawings other than the one you’re currently working on: I know that one’s going to come in handy.</p>  <p>Here’s a quick run-down of the main new features…</p>  <p><strong>Snap to geometric center</strong></p>  <p>A new object snap mode – geometric center, or GCE at the command-line – allows you to snap to the center of closed polylines, whether regular (such as the ones shown below) or irregular.</p>  <p>   <br /><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c7669ea6970b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Snap to geometric center" height="68" src="/assets/image_570009.jpg" style="float: none; margin-left: auto; display: block; margin-right: auto" title="Snap to geometric center" width="448" /></a> </p>  <p><strong>Revision cloud creation</strong></p>  <p>The REVCLOUD command has some new options: you can easily create rectangular or polygonal revision clouds, for instance, as well as modify them afterwards.</p>  <p>   <br /><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b8d0f024e2970c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Revcloud" height="236" src="/assets/image_992419.jpg" style="float: none; margin-left: auto; display: block; margin-right: auto" title="Revcloud" width="440" /></a> </p>  <p><strong>Smart dimensioning</strong></p>  <p>The DIM command now infers the kind of dimension you want to create as you hover over/select geometry.</p>  <p>   <br /><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c7669eab970b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Dim" height="330" src="/assets/image_924814.jpg" style="float: none; margin-left: auto; display: block; margin-right: auto" title="Dim" width="440" /></a> </p>  <p>You’ll also see enhancements around wrapping of long dimension text in this release.</p>  <p><strong>System variable monitor</strong></p>  <p>This feature is intended for users to make sure they don’t have impolite apps or scripts running that don’t reset sysvars to their previous settings when they’ve finished what they’re doing. But this is also likely to be a very handy debugging tool for application developers to identify when their applications are misbehaving in this respect.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b8d0f00a15970c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Sysvar monitor" border="0" height="272" src="/assets/image_448672.jpg" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" title="Sysvar monitor" width="295" /></a></p>  <p><strong>Higher quality graphics display</strong></p>  <p>Those of you with capable graphics cards will see some nice improvements in terms of the display of curves in AutoCAD 2016. We’ve gone beyond the line-smoothing work in 2015 to make greater use of the GPU for elaboration of curves and line-weights.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c76685de970b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Line quality in 2016" border="0" height="206" src="/assets/image_295398.jpg" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" title="Line quality in 2016" width="394" /></a></p>  <p><strong>Coordination models</strong></p>  <p>You can now attach Navisworks and BIM360 models (.NWC, .NWD) directly into AutoCAD using the new CMATTACH command, allowing you to model within and around them. The “BIM underlay” feature team did a great job delivering the ability to attach large models with very good performance.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201bb080a7454970d-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Coordination model" border="0" height="250" src="/assets/image_4045.jpg" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" title="Coordination model" width="454" /></a></p>  <p><strong>Point cloud sectioning and floorplan extraction</strong></p>  <p>Fans of Reality Computing are really going to like AutoCAD’s new 2D floorplan extraction: it’s great to see some point cloud feature extraction capabilities being introduced in the product. The SECTIONPLANE command has been updated to generate the new “slice” section which works with point cloud objects. You can then use the PCEXTRACTSECTION command to generate geometry based on this slice through the point cloud:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c766860a970b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Section extraction options for point clouds" border="0" height="240" src="/assets/image_202330.jpg" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" title="Section extraction options for point clouds" width="302" /></a></p>  <p><strong>PDF enhancements</strong></p>  <p>AutoCAD 2016 creates better PDFs:</p>  <ul>   <li>They are <strong>smaller</strong> – typically half the size or less than the 2015 output – and are generated more <strong>quickly</strong> </li>    <li>All <strong>text</strong> is now searchable and selectable (unless you choose otherwise), even with with multibyte and Unicode characters and SHX fonts (which have the source text added as a comment) </li>    <li><strong>Hyperlinks</strong> are maintained, whether embedded URLs or links between drawing content (e.g. callouts linking to named views) </li>    <li>Publishing sheet sets results in PDFs with named pages, and can be launched directly from the Sheet Set Manager as a one-click option </li>    <li>There are now a number of PC3 files containing <strong>commonly-used output settings</strong>, usable from PLOT, PUBLISH and EXPORTPDF commands </li> </ul>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b8d0f00a47970c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="PDF options" border="0" height="221" src="/assets/image_751155.jpg" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" title="PDF options" width="304" /></a></p>  <ul>   <li>PDF <strong>underlays</strong> now have much better performance </li> </ul>  <p>It’s definitely worth taking another look at AutoCAD’s PDF support if you haven’t in a while.</p>  <p><strong>Rendering improvements</strong></p>  <p>A lot of work has been done on rendering, in this release. We’ve simplified the options available – creating a number of handy presets, such as “Coffee-Break Quality” :-) – and introduced image-based lighting. Many of these enhancements relate to our integration of Autodesk’s RapidRT engine into AutoCAD.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c766861b970b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Render presets palette" border="0" height="238" src="/assets/image_598284.jpg" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" title="Render presets palette" width="204" /></a></p>  <p><strong>Help enhancements</strong></p>  <p>The very handy “UI finder” capability that is built into our user documentation has been extended to find pretty much anything in AutoCAD’s UI, including entries on the app menu and the status bar.</p>  <p>   <br /><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b8d0f02514970c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="UI finder and the app menu" height="389" src="/assets/image_926127.jpg" style="float: none; margin-left: auto; display: block; margin-right: auto" title="UI finder and the app menu" width="300" /></a> </p>  <p>That&#39;s it for this non-exhaustive look at AutoCAD 2016’s new features. Next time we’ll focus more on what the release brings for developers.</p>
