Inspired by a friend who loves this kind of illusion, we managed to put together a version of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pepper&#39;s_ghost" target="_blank">Pepper’s Ghost</a>, to entertain the guests and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trick-or-treating" target="_blank">Trick or Treaters</a>.</p>  <p>It was simple enough: an LCD screen (we had fooled around with a projector, but this worked better), laid down in my daughter’s play-house, with a sheet of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poly(methyl_methacrylate)" target="_blank">Plexiglas</a> angled at 45 degrees to reflect the screen’s image – which from a distance made it look as though it was floating in the air inside the playhouse.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2015392b3ecb3970b-pi" target="_blank"><img align="left" alt="The computer, speakers and screen" border="0" height="151" src="/assets/image_978430.jpg" style="background-image: none;
