<p>On Wednesday morning I hopped on the train to Paris, once again, this time to present at Autodesk France’s third “Soirée de l’Excellence Française”, an invitation-only VIP event for AEC customers in France. (I’d have blogged about it beforehand if the event was open to all, of course.)</p>
<p>I arrived at la Gare de Lyon, as usual, and found an interesting art exhibit outside.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a48c452a200c-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="Give them a hand" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_460245.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Give them a hand" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>I walked next door to the <a href="https://www.citizenm.com/destinations/paris/paris-gare-de-lyon-hotel" rel="noopener" target="_blank">citizenM</a> – part of the <a href="https://www.citizenm.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">chain of hotels</a> I’ve stayed at for AU London, the last <a href="https://www.keanw.com/2018/06/au-london-2018.html" rel="noopener" target="_blank">two</a> <a href="https://www.keanw.com/2019/06/au-london-2019.html" rel="noopener" target="_blank">years</a>. It’s a fun place to stay, although the switch to turn all the lights off doesn’t seem as funny as it might once have, somehow.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4da1c8a200b-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="If only it was that easy" border="0" height="397" src="/assets/image_515238.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="If only it was that easy" width="400" /></a></p>
<p>My room had a nice view across towards the station, and the sky was lovely.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4da1c93200b-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="The Simpsons in Paris" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_204299.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="The Simpsons in Paris" width="375" /></a></p>
<p>I managed to catch up with friends for dinner, and on the way back to the hotel deliberately exited the metro system early to give myself a nice bedtime walk.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4da1c97200b-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="Houseboats in Paris" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_561306.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Houseboats in Paris" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>A few years ago <a href="https://www.keanw.com/2016/11/intelligent-building-systems-in-paris.html" rel="noopener" target="_blank">I participated in the Intelligent Building Systems</a> exhibition in Paris. As this year’s event coincided with the evening event I was in Paris for, I decided to head across for the morning to IBS 2019.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b59967200d-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="IBS" border="0" height="351" src="/assets/image_491505.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="IBS" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>I attended a couple of interesting panel sessions. The first dealt with this question: “<em>Can BIM for Operations become the Digital Twin of a building, for maintenance and technical management purposes?”</em></p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a48c4539200c-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="First panel at IBS on Thursday" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_140960.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="First panel at IBS on Thursday" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>The second panel included my friend <a href="https://www.keanw.com/2019/08/a-visit-to-geneva-with-brunet-saunier-architecture.html" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Jacques Levy-Bencheton</a> as a panellist. This panel dealt with the statement “<em>BIM, a central element of the building lifecycle?”</em></p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b59971200d-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="Jacques during the second IBS panel" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_786763.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Jacques during the second IBS panel" width="375" /></a></p>
<p>At the end of the afternoon I headed across to Hotel Buffon at Rue Cardinal Lemoine 47, the venue for the evening’s event. I walked there from my hotel, which was very nice.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b58ff3200d-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="Jussieu" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_268620.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Jussieu" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Hôtel Buffon is a lovely old building with its own grounds, nestled in central Paris. From what I can tell it’s only available for private (and exclusive) hire.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a48c4548200c-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="Lovely venue" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_446406.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Lovely venue" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>The interior is stunning.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b58ffd200d-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="Piano" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_6077.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Piano" width="375" /></a></p>
<p>The conference room was also decorated very nicely.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4da1ca6200b-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="Lucky chair" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_312673.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Lucky chair" width="375" /></a></p>
<p>Between 80 and 90 people attended the event, a great turnout.<a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4da1caf200b-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="A good crowd" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_152901.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="A good crowd" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Here’s something you won’t see very often: me wearing a jacket.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b5900c200d-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="Suited and booted" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_409284.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Suited and booted" width="327" /></a></p>
<p>I presented the three projects The Living worked on that effectively launched our Generative Design for AEC strategy.<a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a48c4554200c-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="MaRS" border="0" height="229" src="/assets/image_352397.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="MaRS" width="500" /></a></p>

<p>After my 20 minutes of fame, there were a number of customer presentations. First was Thibaut Robert, who talked about <a href="http://traa.fr" rel="noopener" target="_blank">TRAA</a>’s philosophy to engage the senses, focusing on <a href="http://traa.fr/portfolio/74-logements-sociaux-et-3-commerces/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">a stunning housing project</a>.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b59021200d-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="Thibaut Robert from TRAA" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_16952.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Thibaut Robert from TRAA" width="500" /></a></p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a48c4cc4200c-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="I love the way this concrete is finished" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_512216.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="I love the way this concrete is finished" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Next was Luca Dal Cerro from <a href="https://engie-axima.fr/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">ENGIE Axima</a>, whose team – with the help of Autodesk Consulting – created an early prototype using Generative Design for hospitals.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a48c456e200c-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="Luca Dal Cerro from ENGIE Axima" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_253002.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Luca Dal Cerro from ENGIE Axima" width="281" /></a></p>
<p>Luca very kindly credited me with having been involved in this award winning project (it won a prize at a BIM-related awards ceremony, last week). All I’d done was attend a couple of calls, back in March: the actual work was accomplished by those who delivered the prototype. What’s really exciting is that <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assistance_Publique_%E2%80%93_H%C3%B4pitaux_de_Paris" rel="noopener" target="_blank">AP-HP</a> have now asked ENGIE Axima to turn this prototype into a real project.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b5902d200d-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="Engie Axima" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_635051.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Engie Axima" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Next was Annalisa De Maestri from <a href="http://www.v-p.com/en" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Valode et Pistre</a>, who talked about how they’ve used technology to implement some of Paris’s <a href="http://www.v-p.com/en/projects" rel="noopener" target="_blank">most iconic projects</a>.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b59980200d-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="Annalisa De Maestri from Valode et Pistre" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_382339.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Annalisa De Maestri from Valode et Pistre" width="375" /></a></p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a48c456a200c-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="Augmented architecture" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_609356.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Augmented architecture" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>The closing presentation was by the officers of the French chapter of the <a href="https://www.ctbuh.org/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Council on Tall Buildings and Urban Habitat</a>. Ariane Dienstag and Ed Woods (who I’d met when I presented <a href="https://www.keanw.com/2019/07/generatively-paris.html" rel="noopener" target="_blank">a GD session for Bouyges Immobilier, back in July</a>) from CTBUH talked about the history and reach of this organisation, and how it has now established a <a href="https://regions.ctbuh.org/france" rel="noopener" target="_blank">more formal presence in France</a>.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a48c4ccd200c-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="Ariane and Ed" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_399257.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Ariane and Ed" width="375" /></a></p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b5903d200d-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="CTBUH" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_694919.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="CTBUH" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Emmanuel Di Giacomo and David Mourjan – who both played critical roles to make sure this event was a success – wrapped up proceedings before the “cocktail dînatoire”, a nice opportunity to engage with attendees and discuss their plans for using generative design.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a48c4578200c-pi" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img alt="Emmanuel and David" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_417135.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Emmanuel and David" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>I’m very happy (and honoured) to have had the chance to present in front of this audience. I also found the discussions afterwards to be really engaging: people are clearly thinking deeply about this technology and its applications.</p>
