<p>Now for something I hinted at in <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2008/08/through-the-int.html">this previous post</a>...</p> <p>I'm very happy to announce that <em>Through the Interface</em> has two new siblings:</p> <p><a href="http://modthemachine.typepad.com/"><img style="border-top-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px" height="315" alt="Brian Ekin's Mod the Machine" src="/assets/image_229023.jpg's%20Mod%20the%20Machine_1.png" width="378" border="0"></a> </p> <p><em><a href="http://modthemachine.typepad.com/">Mod the Machine</a></em> is a blog devoted to developers working with Autodesk Inventor and is written by Brian Ekins, Inventor API designer.</p> <p><a href="http://thebuildingcoder.typepad.com/"><img style="border-top-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px" height="314" alt="Jeremy Tammik's The Building Coder" src="/assets/image_54733.jpg's%20The%20Building%20Coder_1.png" width="378" border="0"></a> </p> <p><em><a href="http://thebuildingcoder.typepad.com/">The Building Coder</a></em> is a blog devoted to developers working with Autodesk Revit and is written by Jeremy Tammik from our European DevTech team.</p> <p>For those of you working with one of these products, I strongly recommend subscribing to their respective RSS feeds (<a href="http://modthemachine.typepad.com/my_weblog/index.rdf">here</a> for <em>Mod the Machine</em>, and <a href="http://thebuildingcoder.typepad.com/blog/index.rdf">here</a> for <em>The Building Coder</em>).
