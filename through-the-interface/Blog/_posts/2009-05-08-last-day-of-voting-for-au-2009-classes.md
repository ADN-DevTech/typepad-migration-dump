<p>I’ve been a bit remiss posting on our plans for this year’s AU. At AU <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2007/08/autodesk-univer.html">2007</a> and <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2008/07/au-2008-class-s.html">2008</a> my team organised and ran a separate Developer Track. Last year we had already hoped to fold our classes into the Customization &amp; Programming track – mainly to avoid confusion from AU attendees - but it didn’t quite happen. This year it finally has.</p>  <p>Here are the tracks that we would probably have gathered under the Developer Track umbrella, if we were managing it in the same way this year. You will find them all in <a href="http://au.autodesk.com/?nd=au_voting_login">the AU Online voting system</a> under the Customization &amp; Programming track. If you plan on attending this year’s AU, please go to the site and vote for your favourite sessions, as this will increase the chance of them being part of the conference.</p>  <p><strong><em>The voting closes today, May 8th, at midnight Pacific time.</em></strong></p>  <p>[I have marked the “guest presenters” (coming from our Engineering or Consulting teams) in italics, the names of the DevTech presenters are all in standard font.]</p>  <p><strong>Platforms &amp;
