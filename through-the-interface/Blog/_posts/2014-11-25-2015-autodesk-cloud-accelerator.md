<p>Autodesk’s Cloud Platforms team is running an interesting “incubator” program to encourage software developers to create applications that leverage Autodesk’s PaaS layer. (This currently includes the View &amp; Data, AutoCAD I/O, Autodesk Fusion 360, Autodesk BIM 360 and Autodesk ReCap 360 web-service APIs.)</p>
<p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b8d09473fe970c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="San Francisco" border="0" height="201" src="/assets/image_60116.jpg" style="background-image: none; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="San Francisco" width="454" /></a></p>
<p>I won’t go into the details – <a href="http://adndevblog.typepad.com/cloud_and_mobile/2014/11/get-ready-for-the-autodesk-cloud-accelerator-program.html">you can get them here</a> – but I will call out the important points as I see them.</p>
<ul>
<li>2 weeks of working on your cloud-integrated app in Autodesk’s San Francisco office
<ul>
<li><strong>March 9-20, 2015</strong></li>
<li>Autodesk experts on hand to help</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Open to all, but there’s limited capacity
<ul>
<li><strong>14 companies</strong> will be chosen</li>
<li>You need to submit a proposal by <strong>January 10, 2015</strong> to be considered</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Autodesk will cover <strong>accommodation</strong> costs for 1-2 people per company
<ul>
<li>Travel is not covered</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>This is a great opportunity to have Autodesk help you develop a tool that meets a compelling need your customers have for a cloud-integrated application. Not only that, but you’ll have the chance to demo it in front of Autodesk executives at the end of the 2 weeks. That’s really valuable exposure for your work.</p>
