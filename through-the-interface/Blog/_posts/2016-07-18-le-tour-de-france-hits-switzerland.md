<p>The <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2016_Tour_de_France" target="_blank">2016 Tour de France</a> passed within a few hundred metres of my home, this afternoon. It comes to Switzerland every so often – I’m guessing at every 5-6 years, on average – but it’s the first time I’ve had the chance to see it here.</p> <p>We made an afternoon of it: with some friends we headed over to <a href="https://www.google.ch/maps/@47.0207298,7.0307149,3a,75y,257.42h,79.57t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sRp76pR2sfkMQtE7iziz6iw!2e0!7i13312!8i6656?hl=en" target="_blank">a good spot</a> in plenty of time for the kids to decorate the road with some pavement chalk.</p> <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b8d2069b3b970c-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Decorating the road" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 30px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="Decorating the road" src="/assets/image_603040.jpg" width="504" height="285"></a></p> <p>We then waited around for the bicycles to show up. The sponsors’ caravan came first and the kids had plenty of fun collecting goodies from the various 2016 Tour sponsors.</p> <p>A little later the first cyclists appeared, a couple of the <a href="http://www.letour.com/le-tour/2016/us/teams/eqs.html" target="_blank">Etixx-Quick Step team</a> on a breakaway.</p> <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c87cc406970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="The Etixx-Quick Step breakaway" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 30px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="The Etixx-Quick Step breakaway" src="/assets/image_358130.jpg" width="504" height="285"></a></p> <p>The main group followed very shortly after. Here’s the minute or so of intense activity before we called it a day.</p> <p>&nbsp;</p> <p><iframe height="281" src="https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/77OaUz3-fog?rel=0&amp;showinfo=0" frameborder="0" width="500" allowfullscreen></iframe></p> <p>&nbsp;</p> <p align="left">We’ve popped back to our friends’ home, where they’re hosting a post-Tour (or at least a post-Stage 16) barbeque. Here are some special Tour cupcakes they prepared for the occasion (they’ve recently started making cake toppers <a href="http://tiptoppers.ch" target="_blank">commercially</a>, in case anyone’s interested in getting some custom – and edible! – cake designs printed for a party).</p> <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c87cc40a970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Tour cupcakes" style="border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 30px auto; border-left: 0px; display: block; padding-right: 0px" border="0" alt="Tour cupcakes" src="/assets/image_174890.jpg" width="504" height="379"></a></p>
