<p>The countdown is on… the first <a href="http://www.autodesk.com/gallery/design-nights/designnightswitzerland" target="_blank">Design Night Switzerland</a> is being held at <a href="http://www.case-a-chocs.ch" target="_blank">la Case a Chocs</a> in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neuch%C3%A2tel" target="_blank">Neuch<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Neuch%C3%A2tel">âtel</a></a> on October 20th, 2016 to celebrate Autodesk Switzerland’s 25th anniversary. (It’s actually very timely, as la Case has celebrated its own 25th birthday, this year).</p> <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201bb09401a17970d-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Design Night Switzerland flyer" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 30px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="Design Night Switzerland flyer" src="/assets/image_803015.jpg" width="500" height="696"></a></p> <p>The theme for the event is robotics: as I’ve mentioned before on this blog, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jaquet-Droz_automata" target="_blank">some of the first automata</a> – in fact the ones that inspired the movie Hugo – were invented in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Chaux-de-Fonds" target="_blank">la Chaux-de-Fonds</a> (also in the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canton_of_Neuch%C3%A2tel" target="_blank">canton of Neuch<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Neuch%C3%A2tel">â</a>tel</a>), so there’s a long history in the region.
