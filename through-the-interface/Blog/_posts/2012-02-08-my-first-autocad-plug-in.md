<p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20168e6e9ded9970c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="My First Plug-In Training" border="0" height="48" src="/assets/image_944385.jpg" style="background-image: none; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; padding-top: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="My First Plug-In Training" width="479" /></a>Hot on the heels of the Revit and Inventor installments in <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2011/08/the-my-first-plug-in-series.html" target="_blank">this series of guides</a> (OK, OK – perhaps not exactly hot, but then “warm on the heels” doesn’t have quite the same ring to it ;-), I’m happy to announce that the <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2011/07/autocad-net-training-devtv-downloadable-session-recordings.html" target="_blank">previously</a> <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2011/10/recorded-autocad-net-training-available-for-download.html" target="_blank">mentioned</a> “My First AutoCAD Plug-in” guide <a href="http://autodesk.com/myfirstautocadplugin" target="_blank"><strong>is now available</strong></a>.</p>
<p>Stephen Preston, the author of this excellent guide, has done a great job of creating a compelling sample – which keeps your block attributes horizontal, irrespective of the block rotation – and a series of straightforward explanations to help power-users and programming “newbies” take the plunge into the world of AutoCAD programming.</p>
