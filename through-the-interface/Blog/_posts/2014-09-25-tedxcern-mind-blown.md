<p>I was expecting <a href="http://tedxcern.web.cern.ch" target="_blank">yesterday’s TEDx event</a> at <a href="http://home.web.cern.ch" target="_blank">CERN</a> to be good, but it was way beyond that.</p>  <p>The overall theme of the event was “<em>Forward: Charting the Future with Science</em>.” It comprised 17 separate sessions grouped into 3 sections – <em>Adapt</em>, <em>Change</em> &amp; <em>Create</em> – with <strong><a href="http://tedxcern.web.cern.ch/speakers/brian-cox-host" target="_blank">Brian Cox</a></strong> as host.</p>  <p><a href="https://cds.cern.ch/record/1757548/files/cern-60_02_VM3_0153.jpg" target="_blank"><img title="Brian Cox" style="border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; border-left: 0px; display: block; padding-right: 0px" border="0" alt="Brian Cox" src="/assets/image_719196.jpg" width="394" height="264" /></a></p>  <p>The event was planned and executed very well: even when the occasional minor glitch occurred it only ended up adding fun to the proceedings.</p>  <p><a href="http://tedxcern.web.cern.ch/programme" target="_blank">The agenda</a> was structured nicely, with a good progression of themes between sessions. I really liked the inclusion of 3 <a href="http://ed.ted.com" target="_blank">TED-Ed</a> sessions on climate change, matter &amp; anti-matter and cosmic rays, which apparently met the usual requirement for TEDx events also to show material from TED.com.</p>  <p>To give you a sense of the quality of the event, here’s a single sentence on each of the 17 live sessions.</p>  <p><em><font size="4">Adapt</font></em></p>  <p><strong><a href="https://cds.cern.ch/record/1757548/files/cern-60_06_VM4_0130.jpg" target="_blank"><img title="Robert Crease" style="border-top: 0px;
