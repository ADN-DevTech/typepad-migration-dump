<p>On Monday afternoon I flew back across to the Netherlands, this time from Bern airport.</p>
<p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20223c850f23f200c-pi" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"><img alt="Move to the cloud" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_2558.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Move to the cloud" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>My main purpose for this trip was to attend the <a href="http://simaud.org/2018" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Symposium on Simulation for Architecture and Urban Design</a>, or SimAUD, in Delft.</p>
<p>I’ve been staying at an Airbnb a little way out of the centre of town, the advantage being I’m just across from a really pretty park.</p>
<p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20224df38a208200b-pi" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"><img alt="Statue in the park" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_111191.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Statue in the park" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Delft is a university town and is a bit like a smaller, easier version of Amsterdam. There are canals everywhere…</p>
<p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20223c850f24a200c-pi" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"><img alt="Long canal" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_912176.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Long canal" width="500" /></a></p>
<p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20224df38a210200b-pi" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"><img alt="Ugly ducklings" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_528999.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Ugly ducklings" width="500" /></a></p>
<p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20224e03f8f43200d-pi" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"><img alt="Delft is beautiful" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_743228.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Delft is beautiful" width="375" /></a>… and almost everyone seems to travel around by bike:</p>
<p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20224e03f8f4b200d-pi" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"><img alt="Bikes parked on the canal" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_981610.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Bikes parked on the canal" width="500" /></a></p>

<p>You don’t necessarily have to rent bikes by the day, these days: you can also use <a href="https://mobike.com/global/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Mobikes</a> on a ride-by-ride basis. Lorenzo and Danil, colleagues from The Living in New York, have been using these all week:</p>

<p>SimAUD is being hosted by TU Delft, in their Architecture building:</p>
<p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20224e03f9106200d-pi" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"><img alt="Putting the high tech in Architecture" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_293933.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Putting the high tech in Architecture" width="375" /></a></p>
<p>SimAUD was originally started, many moons ago, by members of Autodesk Research (including my manager, Azam Khan). The conference is now completely independent and attracts a few hundred people each year. The conference is currently a really nice size, in that there’s a critical mass of people who make it worth attending, but it’s still a single-track conference (so everyone basically sees the same content).</p>
<p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20223c850f25d200c-pi" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"><img alt="One of the developer-centric sessions" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_37142.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="One of the developer-centric sessions" width="500" /></a></p>
<p><a href="http://simaud.com/2018/program.php" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">The content</a> can be a little heavy for those of us more used to dealing with bits than atoms, but it’s still extremely interesting.</p>
<p>Lorenzo and Danil are presenting a session on Generative Urban Design on Thursday. (They recently presented a <a href="http://labs.blogs.com/its_alive_in_the_lab/2018/06/autodesks-the-living-studio-generative-design-for-architectural-space-planning.html" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">webinar on Generative Design for Architectural Space Planning</a>, in case you want to check it out).</p>
<p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20223c850f262200c-pi" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"><img alt="Lorenzo and Danil working on their presentation" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_453152.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Lorenzo and Danil working on their presentation" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Each day there’s a public keynote in a central space that allows students from TU Delft to attend. The first day’s was by Marilyne Andersen from EPFL, discussing embracing daylighting dynamics in human-driven design.</p>
<p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20223c850f26a200c-pi" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"><img alt="Keynote from Marilyne Andersen from EPFL" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_338011.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Keynote from Marilyne Andersen from EPFL" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>In the evening we met with <a href="http://www.keanw.com/2018/03/blown-away-in-amsterdam.html" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Jelmer Frank from Van Wijnen</a> and headed across to <a href="http://www.restaurantvoc.nl/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Restaurant VOC</a>, a lovely place selected by my colleague Ruslana.</p>
<p>It contained a beautiful spiral staircase that apparently used to be installed in a steamship on the Mississippi.</p>

<p>This morning I came in early and wandered around the architectural workshop. I love how empty university buildings are at 8am.</p>

<p>I’m really happy I’ve had the chance to attend this year’s (and my first) SimAUD. I’m leaving a little early – I’ll just get to see a few sessions on Thursday, the last day – as I’m heading off on a <a href="http://www.keanw.com/2018/05/one-mans-trash-bjarne-stroustrups-the-c-programming-language.html" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">bigger trip</a>, next week, and need to spend some time with my family before I head off.</p>
