<p>When I was in Prague recently I spent some time with Alex Vinckier and Kris Riemslagh from <a href="http://hsbcad.com/" target="_blank">hsbcad</a>, an ADN member and <a href="https://www.facebook.com/hsbcad" target="_blank">provider of software for the offsite construction industry</a>. I’ve corresponded and spoken with Alex and Kris a number of times during my time at Autodesk, but this was my first opportunity to meet them in person. They suggested I coordinate with another member of the hsbcad team – Alex’s twin brother, Karel – to visit to one of their best customers, who happens to be based just 40km from my home.</p>  <p>So it was that Karel and I met (again, for the first time) yesterday afternoon at the factory of <a href="http://www.jpf-ducret.ch" target="_blank">JPF-DUCRET in Orges</a>. We were welcomed by Jean-Marc Ducret, who took the time to present the company and then give us an extended tour of the facility.</p>  <p>JPF-DUCRET have been using AutoCAD in conjunction with hsbCAD to build some incredible <a href="http://www.jpf-ducret.ch/fr/references/refuge-du-gouter.html" target="_blank">wooden structures</a> such as the <a href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Refuge_du_Go%C3%BBter" target="_blank">Refuge du Goûter</a> on <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mont_Blanc" target="_blank">Mont Blanc</a>. Their latest project is to replace the wood for the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Globe_of_Science_and_Innovation" target="_blank">Globe of Science and Innovation</a> at <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CERN" target="_blank">CERN</a>.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c7dcea78970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="The Globe being renovated back in 2009" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="The Globe being renovated back in 2009" src="/assets/image_137612.jpg" width="504" height="319" /></a></p>  <p align="center"><font color="#a5a5a5">photo credit: </font><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/73695971@N00/4054204484"><font color="#a5a5a5">Renovations proceeding at the Globe</font></a><font color="#a5a5a5"> via </font><a href="http://photopin.com"><font color="#a5a5a5">photopin</font></a><font color="#a5a5a5"> </font><a href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-sa/2.0/"><font color="#a5a5a5">(license)</font></a></p>  <p align="center">&#160;</p>  <p>I visited the Globe when <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2014/09/tedxcern-mind-blown.html" target="_blank">I attended TEDxCERN</a>, last year. Interestingly the Globe – which is apparently one of the most-visited tourist destinations in Switzerland – started its life at <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expo.02" target="_blank">Expo.02</a> in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Neuch%C3%A2tel" target="_blank">Neuchâtel</a> as the “<a href="http://www.charpente-concept.com/realisations_details/141/palais-de-lequilibre-ou-globe-de-la-science-et-de-linnovation-geneve-suisse" target="_blank">Palais de l’Equilibre</a>”. Ducret-Orges was involved in the original manufacture of the Globe.</p>  <p>Jean-Marc showed us the model of the Globe inside AutoCAD:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c7dcea80970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="The Globe in AutoCAD" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="The Globe in AutoCAD" src="/assets/image_235651.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c7dcea84970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="A closer view of the model" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="A closer view of the model" src="/assets/image_266705.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a></p>  <p>Touring the factory, we saw the progression from raw materials to finished product. JPF-DUCRET’s use of locally sourced wood allows them to maintain a relatively small amount of stock – this is enough for about 4 days of production, for instance:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c7dcea8c970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="The raw materials" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="The raw materials" src="/assets/image_863196.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a></p>  <p>The wood used for the Globe is actually a bit different; it’s been impregnated, giving it a greenish appearance.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201bb0880e538970d-pi" target="_blank"><img title="The wood for the Globe is impregnated" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="The wood for the Globe is impregnated" src="/assets/image_515630.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a></p>  <p>After leaving storage, the wood gets <a href="http://microtec.eu/en/solutions/all-solutions/first-goldeneye-702-installed-in-switzerland" target="_blank">scanned automatically</a> using X-rays and humidity sensors, allowing it to be sorted and used appropriately.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c7dcea98970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="After being X-rayed and tested for humidity, the wood is sorted" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="After being X-rayed and tested for humidity, the wood is sorted" src="/assets/image_709929.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a></p>  <p>The wood gets marked with a special pen at appropriate locations, allowing another machine to cut it automatically, ready for gluing. Here’s where the pieces are glued into longer planks:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201bb0880e53f970d-pi" target="_blank"><img title="This machine glues pieces together" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="This machine glues pieces together" src="/assets/image_381434.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a></p>  <p>The resultant planks are <em>long</em>.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b8d166d956970c-pi" target="_blank"><img title="So you end up with long pieces" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="So you end up with long pieces" src="/assets/image_752763.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a></p>  <p>These planks get sprayed with more glue, this time for lamination:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c7dceaa0970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="The lengths of wood are sprayed with glue" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="The lengths of wood are sprayed with glue" src="/assets/image_824548.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a></p>  <p>The glue-covered planks are then placed together according to the target form.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c7dceaa9970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Then they get stuck together" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="Then they get stuck together" src="/assets/image_642728.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a></p>  <p>Lamination is performed by massive, manually positioned jigs. Technology exists – that hsbcad can drive – to automatically position such jigs, but Jean-Marc explained that this is one area that didn’t justify the technology investment for JPF-DUCRET: at the scale at which they work it’s more efficient to make use of skilled, manual labour to move the components of the jig to the required positions.</p>  <p>Here’s an empty jig, ready to be filled:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c7dceaae970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="A massive, manually adjusted jig" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="A massive, manually adjusted jig" src="/assets/image_877044.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a></p>  <p>Here’s a view of another – this time full – jig from the opposite end of the factory… a really impressive sight. It reminded me of <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2013/09/a-tour-of-the-factory-at-the-morgan-motor-company.html" target="_blank">my trip to the Morgan factory in the UK</a>, although at a very different scale.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201bb0880e547970d-pi" target="_blank"><img title="These jigs are massive" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="These jigs are massive" src="/assets/image_923023.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a>It’s possible to see the profile of the resulting beams in the laminated planks: to optimise the use of materials, the below planks have been placed ready to for machining down to particular profile.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201bb0880e54d970d-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Here you see a profile of the eventual shape" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="Here you see a profile of the eventual shape" src="/assets/image_586228.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a></p>  <p>JPF-DUCRET have a couple of 5-axis mills that machine the wood to match the model generated using AutoCAD and hsbcad. The mills are driven using <a href="http://www.ddxgroup.com/en/software/easywood" target="_blank">DDX tools</a>.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201bb0880e556970d-pi" target="_blank"><img title="One of the mills at work" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="One of the mills at work" src="/assets/image_866397.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a>Here are the beams once they’ve been machined. I had thought the beams shown previously were destined to be rounded – like the beams on the left of the below image – but I now suspect they ended up looking like the beams to the right (which will be combined with rounded ones to create larger beams with a circular profile).</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c7dceabb970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="This helps see how the shaped beams are formed and composed" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="This helps see how the shaped beams are formed and composed" src="/assets/image_727135.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a></p>  <p>Again, all of this is at an impressive scale:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c7dceac3970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="These babies are big" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="These babies are big" src="/assets/image_146632.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a></p>  <p>After a few more steps, the beams are finished. Here are the first ones ready to be shipped off to CERN:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201bb0880e55e970d-pi" target="_blank"><img title="The completed beams" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="The completed beams" src="/assets/image_473795.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201bb0880e85e970d-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Yes, they&#39;re that long" style="border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; border-left: 0px; display: block; padding-right: 0px" border="0" alt="Yes, they&#39;re that long" src="/assets/image_767941.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a></p>  <p>I hope this post helps give some sense of how interesting this trip was. I’ve always loved wood as a material: for me it was a genuine wonder to see how companies such as JPF-DUCRET use software tools to deliver projects such as this.</p>  <p>It’s clear that people working in this area of the industry are genuinely passionate about what they do, whether they’re working on high-profile projects like the Globe or building schools and homes (JPF-DUCRET also use hsbcad to build much more conventional structures, even if both companies tend to be associated with challenging, highly complex projects).</p>  <p>And it’s great to see decades of patience and dedication paying off… the increased emphasis on sustainable development is helping wood come back into its own as a building material. Which has to be a good thing for both companies.<a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c7dcead2970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Karel Vinckier (left) with Jean-Marc Ducret (right)" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 50px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="Karel Vinckier (left) with Jean-Marc Ducret (right)" src="/assets/image_637199.jpg" width="504" height="337" /></a></p>  <p>Many thanks to Jean-Marc and Karel for making this fascinating visit happen. I’m looking forward to seeing what these companies help create next!</p>
