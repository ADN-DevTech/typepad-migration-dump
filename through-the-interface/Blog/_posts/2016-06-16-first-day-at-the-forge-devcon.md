<p align="left">It was an amazing, whirlwind first day at the first ever Forge DevCon. I was there at 7am to put the finishing touches on the very cool Autodesk VR experience in the exhibit hall. The day’s session kicked off with a keynote by Amar Hanspal and a number of other great presenters.&nbsp; </p> <p align="center"><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c86ea654970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Amar starts the proceedings" style="border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 30px 0px; border-left: 0px; display: inline; padding-right: 0px" border="0" alt="Amar starts the proceedings" src="/assets/image_376006.jpg" width="504" height="285"></a></p> <p>I was really pleased with how our VR stand came together. Merten Stroetzel and Hans Kellner – colleagues in the Office of the CTO – provided their deep technical expertise, while JJ Stott and Clementine Joly did an amazing job with their demos. Here’s the crew getting settled in.</p> <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c86ea65c970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Our event staff testing things out" style="border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 30px auto; border-left: 0px; display: block; padding-right: 0px" border="0" alt="Our event staff testing things out" src="/assets/image_709337.jpg" width="504" height="286"></a></p> <p>A highlight was showing the experience to <a href="http://twitter.com/chr1sa" target="_blank">Chris Anderson</a>. While chatting, Chris had expressed some skepticism about the value of VR, so I suggested giving it a try.</p> <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201bb0912121d970d-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Merten talking to Chris Anderson about VR" style="border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 30px auto; border-left: 0px; display: block; padding-right: 0px" border="0" alt="Merten talking to Chris Anderson about VR" src="/assets/image_392754.jpg" width="285" height="507"></a></p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201bb09121225970d-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Chris gets immersed" style="border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 30px auto; border-left: 0px; display: block; padding-right: 0px" border="0" alt="Chris gets immersed" src="/assets/image_708807.jpg" width="504" height="504"></a></p> <p>Everyone who tried the high-end VRED demo came away “getting it”, Chris included. Which was really cool.</p> <p>Aside from the expo presence, I also helped by coordinating the VR speaker track. The track kicked off with the awesome <a href="https://twitter.com/joshcarpenter" target="_blank">Josh Carpenter</a>, formerly from Mozilla and now from Google, who shared his insights on user experience (UX) design for VR.</p> <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201bb09121229970d-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Josh on-stage at the first VR session" style="border-top: 0px; It was really fun: we did a really large-scale demo, where the whole room joined (or tried to, anyway) a curated VR experience.</p> <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b8d1f87215970c-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Half the room at my own VR session" style="border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 30px auto; border-left: 0px; display: block; padding-right: 0px" border="0" alt="Half the room at my own VR session" src="/assets/image_356826.jpg" width="504" height="285"></a></p> <p>Finishing up the day’s VR talks was <a href="https://twitter.com/auradeluxe" target="_blank">Tony Parisi</a>. This was an absolutely amazing event… I thoroughly enjoyed it from beginning to end. In the end 1,000 people badged in on this first day, which is really fantastic. Onwards to day two!</p>
