<p>This year’s Autodesk Technical Summit had a couple of fantastic parties. After all, the event’s raison d’être is – beyond inspiring employees with excellent technical content, much of which I’ll be going through offline during the coming weeks – to get a bunch of introverts to speak to each other. One was held at <a href="http://www.virginlimitededition.com/en/the-roof-gardens" target="_blank">The Roof Gardens / Babylon</a> – a rooftop venue with live flamingoes in central London (wait, what?) – while the other was held at that venerable Londonian institution <a href="https://www.madametussauds.com" target="_blank">Madame Tussauds</a>.</p> <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c8f798d7970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="A fun party at Madame Tussauds" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 30px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="A fun party at Madame Tussauds" src="/assets/image_979145.jpg" width="500" height="281"></a></p> <p>Madame Tussauds is one of the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madame_Tussauds" target="_blank">most visited tourist attractions in London</a>, and has been for the better part of 200 years. People used to visit to get a sense for how celebrities look in real life, nowadays they go to take selfies for sharing on social media. Which in itself is quite interesting: that a wax museum has managed to continually find relevance in a world chock-full with digital technology. I fully admit I had a lot of fun taking and sharing selfies: <a href="https://twitter.com/search?l=&amp;q=from%3Akeanw%20since%3A2017-05-11%20until%3A2017-05-12&amp;src=typd" target="_blank">you can see many of them here</a>.</p> <p>Towards the end of the evening, a colleague told me about a roped off section of the exhibit housing characters from the Star Wars films. He’d managed to sneak in and take a quick look, and I was determined to do likewise. Initiallly I wondered why it was roped off, but then I realised it was probably a good move: several hundred geeks dosed with alcohol at a Star Wars exhibit? What could possibly go wrong? :-)</p> <p>Anyway, I figured that having one or two surreptitious (and very respectful) visitors wouldn’t be a problem, so I snuck in and took a few photos:</p> <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201bb099ab9df970d-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Tey and BB-8" style="border-left-width: 0px;
