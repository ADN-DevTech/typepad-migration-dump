<p>Bright and early, yesterday morning, I hopped on a train (well, a couple of trains) to get across to Basel for this year’s <a href="https://www.swissbau.ch/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Swissbau</a>, the bienniel construction-related trade show held in Switzerland, and apparently one of the largest in Europe.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4dda797200d-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Catching the train" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_649816.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Catching the train" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>I’d never been before, but thanks to <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:6620967506020495360/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">social media</a> I’d compiled a list of people to see there, and had even <a href="https://twitter.com/keanw/status/1215197824159186944" rel="noopener" target="_blank">coordinated to meet up</a> with my friend and fellow blogger, <a href="https://thebuildingcoder.typepad.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Jeremy Tammik</a>.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4dda4c4200d-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Arriving at Swissbau" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_434450.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Arriving at Swissbau" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>There was quite a bit going on at Swissbau, even outside the exhibition&#0160; hall. At one point I saw some people performing while suspended from a building. And no, that’s not how people typically clean windows in Switzerland. ;-)</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4dda4c6200d-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Cirque du Swissbau" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_177615.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Cirque du Swissbau" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>The centre itself has a very cool feature: a hole that looks like it’s been lined with huge fish-like scales…</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b476a3200c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="The Exhibition Centre" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_993615.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="The Exhibition Centre" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>… and extends all the way through the building.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a5024634200b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Holey moley" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_37230.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Holey moley" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Inside, the Swissbau event is huge, with upwards of 900 exhibitors.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a5024636200b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Levels" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_459700.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Levels" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>There were a lot of more traditional construction-centric vendors, showing off new building materials and ceramics.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a5024638200b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Traditional stuff" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_10119.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Traditional stuff" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Of particular interest to me, at least, were the advances (aesthetically, anyway) in solar panels.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4dda4cf200d-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Solar" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_153611.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Solar" width="375" /></a></p>
<p>And we were in Switzerland, so a little chalet-chic was on display, too.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a502463b200b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Skis" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_617553.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Skis" width="375" /></a></p>
<p>Autodesk had a stand with other software vendors up in Hall 2.2.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a502463f200b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="The Autodesk stand" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_873710.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="The Autodesk stand" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>It was a good opportunity to meet up with colleagues from Switzerland and Germany.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4dda4d3200d-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Jeremy and Daniel" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_888156.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Jeremy and Daniel" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>I even had the good fortune to see Martin von Wartburg, who had retired from Autodesk a few years ago but was also passing by to say Hi to people.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a5024649200b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Kean and Martin" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_727916.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Kean and Martin" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Across from the Autodesk stand was Mensch und Maschine’s (Man and Machine to us anglophones).</p>
<p>Marcel Ludwig from MuM had invited me to the event, so it was nice to see him on the stand.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a502464b200b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Marcel from MuM" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_985981.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Marcel from MuM" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Matthias Wasem from BIM Facility was also there, and proved to me he had <a href="http://dasher360.com" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Dasher 360</a> running with the NEST model. :-)</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b476b5200c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Matthias showing Dasher 360" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_348554.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Matthias showing Dasher 360" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Downstairs in Hall 1.0 there was another interesting area.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a502464d200b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Hall 1.0 Süd" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_658405.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Hall 1.0 Süd" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>There were some industry talks an d panel discussions taking place.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a5024653200b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Talks" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_823477.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Talks" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>And there was an interesting bar area that had a green theme (albeit via plastic plants).</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a5024655200b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Greenwashing" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_80184.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Greenwashing" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>The team from NEST had a stand there, too. I was happy to get the chance to catch up with Reto Largo and meet collaborators from his team.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4dda4e1200d-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="NEST" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_184240.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="NEST" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Jeremy and I went around a number of stands in the Innovation Lab, upstairs in Hall 1.1. I’d had a discussion about digital twins a few months ago with Dr. Wissam Wahbeh from FHNW.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4dda4e7200d-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Wissam from FHNW" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_785724.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Wissam from FHNW" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>I was really impressed to see that since then his team had created a really interesting 3D-printed physical mockup of a digital twin connected to IoT sensors. They’d even integrated an LCD screen into the floor to display simulated shadows.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4dda4e9200d-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="A physical digital twin" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_678705.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="A physical digital twin" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Digital twins were everywhere. We stopped by – this time with Claus Mueller – a stand being manned by former Autodesk colleague Matthias Liechti.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b476bd200c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Claus and Jeremy with Matthias" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_945372.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Claus and Jeremy with Matthias" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Matthias’s startup has been working on some digital twin concepts using Forge.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a502465f200b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Demoing digital twin concepts using Forge" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_674342.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Demoing digital twin concepts using Forge" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>We met with Markus Giera and André van der Westhuize from Kaulquappe, who spoke very positively about the business they have built around Forge.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a5024665200b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Kaulquappe" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_788331.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Kaulquappe" width="375" /></a></p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4dda4ec200d-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Again with Forge" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_364904.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Again with Forge" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Nearby was the Mobbot stand. I’d met Agnes from Mobbot at Rob|Arch 2018, and it was nice to reconnect. They’re 3D printing concrete – via a spraying system – for infrastructure projects.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b476c3200c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Agnes from Mobbot" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_970585.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Agnes from Mobbot" width="375" /></a></p>
<p>I also managed to speak with Manuel Frey and Jack Riley from Gruner…</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b476c5200c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Gruner" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_181340.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Gruner" width="375" /></a></p>
<p>… as well as Davis Kantor from CRB.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4dda4f1200d-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="CRB" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_662538.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="CRB" width="375" /></a></p>
<p>Away from the Innovation Lab, I had the chance to see some work being done by André Corpataux from Securiton, who is once again using Forge to create digital twins, this time for display of information relating to physical security.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b476c9200c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="André from Securiton" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_856693.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="André from Securiton" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Despite all this cool digital innovation, it was still reassuring to see the physical world very much present at the event.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4b476cf200c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Scale models" border="0" height="500" src="/assets/image_216146.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Scale models" width="375" /></a></p>
<p>People do still love to see scale models, after all.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a5024677200b-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Tiny houses" border="0" height="375" src="/assets/image_273105.jpg" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" title="Tiny houses" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>I was very happy with my whirlwind tour of Swissbau 2020: it was a really eye-opening experience, and a great opportunity to [re]connect with people in the industry in Switzerland. It’s definitely an event I’ll be making a point of visiting in future.<a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4dda799200d-pi"><br /></a></p>
