<p>On Sunday morning I booked an Uber to get across to Bangalore airport for my flight to Pune. It was still early, so
  the only other being in the street was a cow.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4d3b050200d-pi" target="_blank"><img width="500" height="375" title="Cow" style="margin: 30px auto; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" alt="Cow" src="/assets/image_621714.jpg" border="0"></a></p>
<p>It was busier when I arrived at Bangalore airport.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4d3b059200d-pi" target="_blank"><img width="500" height="375" title="Airport" style="margin: 30px auto; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" alt="Airport" src="/assets/image_744868.jpg" border="0"></a></p>
<p>And more Christmassy, too.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4f859f8200b-pi" target="_blank"><img width="375" height="500" title="Xmas" style="margin: 30px auto; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" alt="Xmas" src="/assets/image_66245.jpg" border="0"></a></p>
<p>I got myself some <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idli" target="_blank">idli sambar</a> (the breakfast is better at the domestic terminal than the international one, by all
  accounts), and then waited by the gate for my SpiceJet flight.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4d3b062200d-pi" target="_blank"><img width="500" height="375" title="Gate" style="margin: 30px auto; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" alt="Gate" src="/assets/image_16160.jpg" border="0"></a></p>
<p>The flight was slightly delayed, but basically uneventful – it takes a little over an hour to get from Bangalore to
  Pune.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4aa8e38200c-pi" target="_blank"><img width="500" height="375" title="Welcome" style="margin: 30px auto; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" alt="Welcome" src="/assets/image_407726.jpg" border="0"></a></p>
<p>This was my first time to Pune, which sometimes comes as a surprise to people. Pune is the CAD capital of Bangalore: most CAD and simulation vendors have offices in the city. Autodesk now has a very large one there (although I wasn’t visiting it on this trip). I’d lived in Bangalore in 2003-2005 and had certainly interviewed a number of candidates from Pune, but somehow hadn’t had a reason to visit until now.</p><p>Nem from the Centre for Computational Technologies (CCTech) picked me up at the airport and drove me to the hotel.
  I’d already heard it from a number of people, but Pune feels a lot like Bangalore did 30 years ago. Before the IT
  revolution led to explosive growth and all that came with it. Although in fairness it was also Sunday afternoon, so
  there was probably less traffic than on a weekday.<a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4aa8e40200c-pi" target="_blank"><img width="500" height="375" title="Pune" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" alt="Pune" src="/assets/image_752318.jpg" border="0"></a></p>
<p>The hotel I’d been booked into was very comfortable. It’s standard practice at hotels and restaurants in the area –
  presumably since the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2008_Mumbai_attacks" target="_blank">Mumbai bombings</a> –
  to have metal detectors at their entrances.</p>
<p>I remember this from Bangalore, too… they’re typically just to pay lip-service to regulations and in most cases the
  people using the equipment (especially the magic bomb-detecting wands that beep like mad when they scan your computer
  bag but are somehow safe to ignore) probably wouldn’t know what to do if they did find a bomb. Certainly the checks of
  cars’ underneaths (using mirrors) as well as the engine compartments and boots/trunks seem very cursory.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4aa8e44200c-pi" target="_blank"><img width="500" height="375" title="Arrival" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" alt="Arrival" src="/assets/image_671213.jpg" border="0"></a></p>
<p>It’s not often you get to see scanning equipment from this side, though. Well, unless you work at airport security.
</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4d3b06d200d-pi" target="_blank"><img width="375" height="500" title="Metal non-detector" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" alt="Metal non-detector" src="/assets/image_187964.jpg" border="0"></a></p>
<p>The hotel was also feeling Christmassy.</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4aa8e50200c-pi" target="_blank"><img width="375" height="500" title="Hotel" style="margin: 30px auto; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" alt="Hotel" src="/assets/image_563004.jpg" border="0"></a></p>
<p>On check-in I found I’d been upgraded to one of the hotel’s six suites. Sweet, indeed!</p>
<p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4d3b07b200d-pi" target="_blank"><img width="500" height="375" title="Suite" style="margin: 30px auto; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" alt="Suite" src="/assets/image_917094.jpg" border="0"></a></p>
<p>After a brief sit-down, Sandip picked me up with Nem and Vijay – the CCTech management team – to go for an excursion
  to the nearby hills. (Sandip and Vijay founded CCTech, back in 2006 or so, and are CEO and CTO respectively.)</p>

Apparently it’s an organisation that does corporate team-building etc. in a pseudo-military environment.</p><p><a href="https://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20240a4d3b2bd200d-pi" target="_blank"><img width="500" height="375" title="Data" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" alt="Data" src="/assets/image_356369.jpg" border="0"></a></p><p>There was another one further up that said “counter-terrorism unit” on the side, which was perhaps a little

  role at Autodesk Research – but this is the first time I’ve had the pleasure of visiting them in Pune. I
  certainly received a warmer-than-warm welcome!</p>
