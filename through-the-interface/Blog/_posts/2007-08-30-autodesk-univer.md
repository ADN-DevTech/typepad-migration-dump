<p>As mentioned <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2007/04/my_team_is_curr.html">some time ago</a>, my team has been working with members of our Engineering teams to put together a comprehensive &quot;Developer&quot; track for AU 2007. We have 52 sessions devoted to developers or people customizing our products, in addition to a further 96 sessions in the &quot;Customization and Programming&quot; track. This adds up to host of great content for developers at this year's Autodesk University.</p>

<p>Below are the details of the various sessions in the &quot;Developer&quot; track. I've marked the course IDs of sessions that have already sold out in <span style="COLOR: red">red</span> and the guest speakers from Engineering in <em>italics</em>. There are three panel discussions where you can come along and get your development-oriented questions answered by experts from DevTech and Engineering. The sessions are filling up quickly (including the ones I'm presenting, thankfully :-) so please sign up soon.</p>

<p>I've created a &quot;Through the Interface&quot; group in <a href="http://au.autodesk.com/2007/login/">AU Online</a> (go to the Network tab). For those of you attending AU, please join the group. I'm looking forward to meeting many of you at AU!</p>

<p>Before I sign off, a quick reminder about <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2007/07/coming-in-septe.html">our DevCamps</a>... we have our <a href="http://www.autodesk.com/AEC-DevCamp">AEC DevCamp</a> next week and our <a href="http://www.autodesk.com/Manufacturing-DevCamp">Manufacturing DevCamp</a> later in September. There is still time to sign up (depending on when you're reading this, of course :-).</p>
