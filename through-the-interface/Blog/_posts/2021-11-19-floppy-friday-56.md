<p>In <a href="https://youtu.be/1Nc01vVC_78" rel="noopener" target="_blank">this week’s episode</a> we play 4 games on a Commodore 64, loaded from 5 1/4” floppy disk. We started with a game I had high hopes for – coming from Gremlin Graphics, and being based on <a href="https://www.amazon.co.uk/Master-Valley-Two-Player-Fantasy-Gamebook/dp/B00195EB5K/ref=sr_1_1" rel="noopener" target="_blank">a series of “fighting fantasy” gamebooks</a> (although not <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fighting_Fantasy" rel="noopener" target="_blank">the Steve Jackson &amp; Ian Livingston ones,</a> it seems) – called <a href="https://www.lemon64.com/games/details.php?ID=3165" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Blood Valley</a>. We then played <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pac-Land" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Pac-Land</a>, an interesting game called <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ATF_(video_game)" rel="noopener" target="_blank">ATF (Advanced Tactical Fighter)</a> and then <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thrust_(video_game)" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Thrust II</a>.</p>
