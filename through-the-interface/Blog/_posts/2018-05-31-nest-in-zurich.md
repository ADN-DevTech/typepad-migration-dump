<p>Yesterday I headed across to Zürich<em> </em>for a number of different meetings. One that I wanted to talk about on this blog is my visit to <a href="https://www.empa.ch/web/empa" target="_blank">Empa</a>, the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swiss_Federal_Laboratories_for_Materials_Science_and_Technology" target="_blank">Swiss Federal Laboratories for Materials Science and Technology</a>, and – in particular – their <a href="https://www.empa.ch/web/nest/overview" target="_blank">NEST</a> building. NEST is a testbed and showcase for emerging techniques in the construction and operation of buildings. They have a heavy focus on modularization, pre-fabrication and robotics.</p><p>I’d been invited to visit by Reto Largo, Director of NEST, along with Philipp Mueller from Autodesk’s Education team.
