<p>There’s a <em>real</em>ly <a href="http://www.real2015.com" target="_blank">interesting conference</a> in San Francisco, later this month (groan). It’s being held in <a href="http://www.real2015.com/#where" target="_blank">Fort Mason</a> on February 25-27, and covers all aspects of <a href="http://realitycomputing.typepad.com/autodesk/about.html" target="_blank">reality computing</a>, whether relating to capture, compute or create:</p>
<p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b8d0cd27fe970c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="Real Conference 2015" border="0" height="207" src="/assets/image_617311.jpg" style="background-image: none; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 40px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-width: 0px;" title="Real Conference 2015" width="465" /></a>Autodesk is the main organizer of the event, but there are <a href="http://www.real2015.com/#partners" target="_blank">lots of other companies and institutions involved, too</a>. Check out the <a href="http://www.real2015.com/#talk" target="_blank">list of speakers</a>: there are executives and specialists from Autodesk lined up as well as several eminent names from the industry.</p>
