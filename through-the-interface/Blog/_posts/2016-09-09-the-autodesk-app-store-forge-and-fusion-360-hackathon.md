<p>Hot on the heels of <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2016/09/hologuide-an-autodesk-hackathon-project.html" target="_blank">our own, internal Hackathon</a> (although, strictly speaking, this one started before it) there’s another external Hackathon running until the end of October. This one’s about developing apps using Forge or Fusion 360 and submitting said apps to the Autodesk App Store.</p> <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c8913070970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Forge Hackathon" style="border-left-width: 0px; border-right-width: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom-width: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 30px auto; display: block; padding-right: 0px; border-top-width: 0px" border="0" alt="Forge Hackathon" src="/assets/image_320624.jpg" width="504" height="254"></a></p> <p>The event really has two distinct parts:  <ol> <li>A series of webinars and Q&amp;A sessions where you can learn about Forge and Fusion 360 development  <ul> <li>These are free and open to all: there’s absolutely no commitment needed to attend</li></ul> <li>An online Hackathon with a financial incentive for you to submit your Forge or Fusion 360 app to the Autodesk App Store  <ul> <li>We’re offering $500 per eligible app</li></ul></li></ol> <p>You can get the full details <a href="http://autodeskforge.devpost.com" target="_blank">here</a>. While the event started on September 1st, the <a href="http://autodeskforge.devpost.com/details/webinars" target="_blank">webinars</a> start on September 20th, in case you’re just interested in learning more about Forge or Fusion 360.The webinars will also be recorded, in case the timing doesn’t work for you. And if you have any questions, be sure to <a href="mailto:forgehackathon@autodesk.com">send the organisers an email</a>.</p>
