<p>We left Singapore from Changi airport on Tuesday night. I always enjoy flying from Changi: there’s so much to see and do, and they have some great traveller-oriented features such as security at each gate rather than in a central location. Our kids came across a Kinect-based experience where you could see your outline projected onto a large screen with physical (i.e. not electronic) pixels. Like a huge e-Ink display, I suppose.</p><p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c936e46b970b-pi" target="_blank"><img width="500" height="375" title="Kinect-based fun at Changi airport" style="margin: 30px auto; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" alt="Kinect-based fun at Changi airport" src="/assets/image_891646.jpg" border="0"></a></p><p>Our next destination was <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coimbatore" target="_blank">Coimbatore</a>. Coimbatore is a smaller Indian city that had two main benefits as a stop on our tour: it has direct flights from Singapore and is a relatively short (4-5 hours) drive to our chosen destination of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kodaikanal" target="_blank">Kodaikanal</a>. We stayed for just one night in Coimbatore, mainly was we arrived quite late and it was going to take so long to travel up to Kodaikanal.</p><p>Here’s a picture of a fishpond in our Coimbatore hotel’s lobby:</p><p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c936e475970b-pi" target="_blank"><img width="500" height="375" title="Don't be coy" style="margin: 30px auto; border: 0px currentcolor; border-image: none; float: none; display: block; background-image: none;" alt="Don't be coy" src="/assets/image_210642.jpg" border="0"></a></p><p>We first visited Kodaikanal back when we were based in Bangalore in 2003, and really wanted to make a follow-on trip here during this tour with our children. Kodaikanal is an interesting town: it’s a hill station, which means it’s often quite cool up here, and it definitely has a special feel to it. It has a lake, and they make cheese and chocolate. So yes, it does sound a bit like Switzerland. :-)</p><p>The drive up to Kodaikanal – for which we hired a car with a driver – was thankfully uneventful.</p><p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c936e483970b-pi" target="_blank"><img width="500" height="375" title="Waiting for a bus" style="margin: 30px auto;
