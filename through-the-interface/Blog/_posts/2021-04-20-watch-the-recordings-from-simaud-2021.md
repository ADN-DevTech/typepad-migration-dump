<p>As mentioned <a href="https://www.keanw.com/2021/04/attend-simaud-2021-for-free.html" target="_blank">last week,</a> this year’s Symposium on <a href="http://www.simaud.org/" target="_blank">Simulation for Architecture and Urban Design (SimAUD)</a> was held on Friday and Saturday of last week (with some pre-conference workshops on the Thursday).</p><p>Autodesk has supported SimAUD since its inception – in fact a number of the people who started the conference were working for Autodesk Research at the time – and we continue to be a proud sponsor of the event, I’m pleased to say. It’s great that our sponsorship helped this year’s SimAUD be free to attend, as it encouraged record numbers of registrants (500+!) and attendees.</p><p>If you weren’t able to attend, then not to worry: the whole event was live-streamed on YouTube and you can catch up on all the non-social sessions from there. In fact that’s a large part of what I’ve been doing today and expect to continue doing tomorrow.</p><p>As it’s a little tricky to find the recordings of the sessions you’re interested in (you can <a href="http://www.simaud.org/2021/program.php" target="_blank">see the program here</a>, in case), I decided to go through and link to the relevant locations in the live-stream(s) from a blog post.
