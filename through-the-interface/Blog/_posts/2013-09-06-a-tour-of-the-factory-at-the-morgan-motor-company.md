<p>As <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2013/08/heading-to-the-uk.html" target="_blank">mentioned</a> <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2013/08/first-day-at-the-morgan-motor-company.html" target="_blank">previously</a>, I spent much of last week visiting an Autodesk customer in the UK, the Morgan Motor Company. They’re based in the beautiful <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Midlands_(region)" target="_blank">West Midlands</a> town of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Malvern" target="_blank">Great Malvern</a> which lies at the foot of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malvern_Hills" target="_blank">the Malvern Hills</a>, an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Area_of_Outstanding_Natural_Beauty" target="_blank">area of outstanding natural beauty</a> (yes, that’s an official designation :-).</p>  <p><em>[<strong>Update:</strong> to clarify, Malvern is in the county of </em><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Worcestershire" target="_blank"><em>Worcestershire</em></a><em> which is in the West Midlands region (not to be confused with </em><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Midlands_(county)" target="_blank"><em>the similarly named county</em></a><em>). So I should probably have said “the beautiful Worcestershire town of…”. Apologies for having stated this in a potentially confusing manner.]</em></p>  <p>To give you some background to Morgan’s use of Autodesk software, I recommend watching <a href="http://au.autodesk.com/au-online/classes-on-demand/class-catalog/2012/autodesk-showcase/morgan-motor-company-maximizing-workflow-efficiency" target="_blank">this recording of Jon Wells’ (Morgan’s head designer) excellent presentation at AU 2012</a>. Last Wednesday Jon kindly took the time to show me around the factory – to a few places not normally visited on the standard factory tour, which is covered in today’s post – and also answered a number of questions I’ll be posting here in interview format when I get the chance to write them all up.</p>  <p>The Morgan Motor Company is an institution in the UK: it’s the last independent British car manufacturer. What I particularly like about the company is that they combine the use of state-of-the-art technology with traditional manufacturing techniques. They employ a local workforce of around 170 people to hand-build cars in a very sustainable way using natural materials. Every Morgan car that leaves the production line has been handcrafted in ash wood, aluminium and leather by highly-skilled specialists.</p>  <p>Let’s take a look at some photos I snapped when on a tour of the factory given by the company’s archivist, Martyn Webb (who also wrote <a href="http://www.amazon.com/Morgan-Malvern-Motoring-Martyn-Webb/dp/1847970397" target="_blank">an excellent book on the history of Morgan Motor Company</a>, in case you’re interested in picking one up :-).</p>  <p>Something that I hadn’t realised before the tour – but that makes perfect sense – is that not all the assembly is now done on-site.
