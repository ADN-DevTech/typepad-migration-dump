<p>For those of you who may have missed it, I <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2012/12/midlife-crisis-planning.html" target="_blank">really</a> <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2013/03/geneva-motor-show-2013.html" target="_blank">like</a> the <a href="http://www.morgan-motor.co.uk" target="_blank">Morgan Motor Company</a>, and in particular the latest incarnation of their iconic <a href="http://www.morgan3wheeler.co.uk" target="_blank">Morgan 3 Wheeler</a>. Which is why I was very happy to see it featured during Autodesk’s recent 2014 launch event:</p>
<br /><iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="264" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/w-kxV8C4xro?start=814&amp;rel=0" width="470"></iframe>  <br />  <br />
<p>What’s fascinating to me is the use of a high-tech design process to create something – which is then built by hand, with a wooden chassis – that has such a low-tech, no frills driving experience:</p>
<br /><iframe allowfullscreen="allowfullscreen" frameborder="0" height="264" src="http://www.youtube.com/embed/YmD_YdLl_X0?rel=0" width="470"></iframe>  <br />  <br />
<p>I’m also a big reader of science fiction, so the idea of donning <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steampunk" target="_blank">steampunk</a> aviator goggles and leather helmet and taking this out for a spin is very appealing indeed.</p>
