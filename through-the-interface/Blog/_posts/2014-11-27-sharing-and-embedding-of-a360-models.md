<p>In advance of next week’s AU, our Cloud Platforms team has released <a href="http://autodesk.typepad.com/a360/2014/11/new-a360-updates-make-it-even-easier-to-create-share-and-upload.html">a new version of A360</a>. The key feature of this update is the ability to share and embed A360 models.</p>  <p>I uploaded a new model to my <a href="http://autodesk360.com">A360</a> account – it works with existing models, too, of course, but it’s nice to show something different – and can already see the new “Share” button at the bottom of the model’s description in the activity feed. (The button is also available at the top of the viewer screen and as an icon in the data view, in case.)</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c71147f2970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Activity feed in A360" style="border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; border-left: 0px; display: block; padding-right: 0px" border="0" alt="Activity feed in A360" src="/assets/image_197972.jpg" width="462" height="265" /></a></p>  <p>When you use the “Share Item” option, A360 brings up a tabbed dialog presenting the options for doing so.</p>  <p>To start with you can copy &amp; paste a shortened link:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c71147f8970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Share a link" style="border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; border-left: 0px; display: block; padding-right: 0px" border="0" alt="Share a link" src="/assets/image_278107.jpg" width="352" height="285" /></a></p>  <p>Next you can email a link to one or more recipients:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201b7c71147fe970b-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Send an email" style="border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; float: none; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 0px; margin: 20px auto; border-left: 0px; display: block; padding-right: 0px" border="0" alt="Send an email" src="/assets/image_580862.jpg" width="354" height="331" /></a></p>  <p>Both these options provide the ability to require a password and allow downloading of the model.</p>  <p>And finally – and this is manna for bloggers – you can generate the HTML fragment to embed the viewer directly in a web-page:</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e201bb07b65e9e970d-pi" target="_blank"><img title="Embed a viewer" style="border-top: 0px; The consumer of the content doesn’t even have to know anything (even the existence of) A360. <a href="http://autodesk360.com">Give it a try</a>!</p>
