<p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e20147e33360a0970b-pi"><img align="left" alt="MS-DOS" border="0" height="86" src="/assets/image_117664.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 0px 20px 0px 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; float: left; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="MS-DOS" width="73" /></a>After many years of absence from the platform, AutoCAD is returning to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MS-DOS" target="_blank">MS-DOS</a>. This is great news for our DOS-based user community, who have been struggling along with R13 for the last decade and a half.</p>  <p>Enabled by the application re-architecture performed for <a href="http://autodesk.com/autocadformac" target="_blank">AutoCAD for Mac</a> (codenamed “Sledgehammer”), the new AutoCAD for DOS (codenamed “Screwdriver”) will deliver compelling performance with a streamlined user interface experience.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2014e604beb25970c-pi" target="_blank"><img alt="AutoCAD for DOS" border="0" height="303" src="/assets/image_281310.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; margin-left: auto; border-top: 0px; margin-right: auto; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="AutoCAD for DOS" width="468" /></a></p>  <p>AutoCAD for DOS will automatically import any CUI-based customizations, replacing ribbons and other Windows-centric UI elements with equivalent pull-down, screen and icon menus.</p>  <p><a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83452464869e2014e604beb5d970c-pi" target="_blank"><img align="right" alt="AutoCAD Tablet" border="0" height="200" src="/assets/image_394040.jpg" style="background-image: none; border-bottom: 0px; border-left: 0px; margin: 0px 0px 0px 20px; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: inline; float: right; border-top: 0px; border-right: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="AutoCAD Tablet" width="144" /></a>For those of you wanting to hook up a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphics_tablet" target="_blank">digitizing tablet</a> to complete the “retro” experience, be sure to look out for our “AutoCAD Tablet” app on the <a href="http://www.apple.com/ipad/from-the-app-store/" target="_blank">Apple AppStore</a>, which will turn your iPad into a wireless graphics tablet for AutoCAD for DOS.</p>  <p>And thanks to this new, flexible core architecture, I have every confidence that it won’t belong before we see AutoCAD for <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OS/2" target="_blank">OS/2</a>, AutoCAD for <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windows_3.1x" target="_blank">WFW 3.11</a> and even AutoCAD for <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CP/M" target="_blank">CP/M</a>. Watch this space. Probably around the same time next year. Actually quite probably this very day, next year. ;-)</p>
