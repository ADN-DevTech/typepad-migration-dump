<p>As a follow-up to <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2011/05/adn-devcast-episode-6-autoloader.html" target="_blank">their session on the Autoloader</a>, AutoCAD 2012’s simplified deployment mechanism, Stephen and Fenton have recorded another DevCast focused on creating an installer to deploy apps via the Autoloader.</p>  <p><a href="http://download.autodesk.com/media/adn/DevCasts/ADN_DevCast_Episode_7.html" target="_blank"><img style="background-image: none; border-right-width: 0px; margin: 20px auto; padding-left: 0px; padding-right: 0px; display: block; float: none; border-top-width: 0px; border-bottom-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; padding-top: 0px" title="The Stephen and Fenton Show - ADN DevCast Episode 7" border="0" alt="The Stephen and Fenton Show - ADN DevCast Episode 7" src="/assets/image_467614.jpg" width="479" height="315" /></a></p>  <p>These two DevCasts are likely to be of relevance to anyone wishing to deploy their plugin via <a href="http://through-the-interface.typepad.com/through_the_interface/2011/06/companion-apps-now-available-in-autocad-2012.html" target="_blank">the “Apps” tab in Autodesk Exchange</a>. There’s also <a href="http://autodesk.com/developapps" target="_blank">a new Developer Center</a> focused on this very task, providing the guidelines and requirements for publishing apps along with a couple of helpful DevTV sessions to get you started.</p>
