<p>In <a href="https://youtu.be/MpWNbMcb2gI" rel="noopener" target="_blank">this week’s episode</a> we play five more fun (or less fun) Commodore 64 games, loaded from floppy diskettes. We start with a pro wrestling game called <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock%27n_Wrestle" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Rock ‘n’ Wrestle</a>, followed by a couple of platform games: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miner_2049er" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Miner 2049er</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mr._Do%27s_Castle" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Mr. Do’s Castle</a>. We then played <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eddie_Kidd_Jump_Challenge" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Eddie Kidd Jump Challenge</a> and wrapped up with an educational game called <a href="https://csdb.dk/release/?id=68351" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Quick Thinking! Robot Tables</a>.</p>
